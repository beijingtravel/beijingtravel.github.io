Hello, I'm Maximus! I’m an IT department executive by profession, and an avid traveler by passion. Currently, I live and work in Beijing, a city that has quickly become a second home for me. While my days are spent overseeing technology solutions and guiding teams to success in the fast-paced IT world, my heart often finds peace in exploring the historical and modern landscapes of this incredible city.


As an executive in the IT field, I have a keen understanding of how technology shapes our world. My role allows me to not only work on innovative projects but also to lead a talented team dedicated to providing cutting-edge solutions. The fast-paced and ever-evolving nature of the IT industry is a thrilling challenge, and I’m constantly inspired by the opportunities technology offers to drive progress and improve lives.


However, despite my busy work schedule, I’ve always had a deep love for travel. There’s something about exploring new places, understanding different cultures, and immersing myself in the beauty of the world that excites me. This passion is what initially drew me to Beijing—a city that beautifully blends tradition with innovation. The harmony of its ancient culture and cutting-edge modernity fascinates me, and I have embraced every opportunity to discover the city’s hidden gems.


Exploring Beijing’s Iconic Landmarks


Living in Beijing has allowed me to explore its most iconic landmarks. I’m fortunate to visit these places frequently, as they each offer a unique connection to China’s history and culture. The Forbidden City, once the home of Chinese emperors, is one of my favorites. Every time I walk through its massive gates, I am reminded of the incredible history that shaped this great nation. The architectural beauty of the Forbidden City never fails to amaze me, and I always find something new to appreciate with each visit.


The Great Wall of China is another landmark that holds a special place in my heart. Whether I’m hiking along its rugged sections or taking in the views from the more restored areas, I am always in awe of its sheer scale and the determination it took to build such a structure. I love the feeling of standing on top of the Wall, looking out over the surrounding mountains, and imagining the soldiers who once guarded it centuries ago.


Beyond the Famous Sights


While the well-known attractions are undeniably impressive, I also love discovering the quieter, lesser-known parts of Beijing. The city is full of hidden treasures, from its charming hutongs (traditional alleyways) to the artistic energy of the 798 Art District. These areas allow me to see a different side of Beijing, one that is rich in local life and creativity.


I am also fond of visiting Beihai Park, a peaceful retreat in the heart of the city. It’s the perfect spot to relax, unwind, and reflect, especially after a busy workday. The park’s beautiful lake, ancient temples, and quiet paths offer a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life.


A Personal Connection to Travel


For me, travel is more than just sightseeing. It’s about connecting with the places I visit on a deeper level. Whether I’m learning about the history of the Temple of Heaven, enjoying a boat ride on Kunming Lake at the Summer Palace, or exploring Beijing’s less-traveled spots, I always aim to immerse myself fully in the experience. It’s a way for me to understand the cultural and historical fabric of a place, as well as to connect with myself in the process.


As an IT professional, my work revolves around creating connections—whether it’s through software, systems, or networks. I see travel in a similar light: it’s all about forming connections, both with the places I visit and the people I meet along the way. Beijing has provided me with countless opportunities to build these connections, and I am grateful for every experience.


My Travel Philosophy


My love for travel is rooted in my belief that exploring new places broadens our horizons and deepens our understanding of the world. Travel teaches us to appreciate different cultures, histories, and perspectives, and it often leads to personal growth. I carry this philosophy with me in both my professional and personal life. Just as I strive to lead with innovation and creativity in my IT work, I seek the same qualities in my travels, always aiming to discover something new, unexpected, and inspiring.


Through this blog, I hope to share my experiences in Beijing, offering a mix of travel tips, personal reflections, and insights into this incredible city. Whether you’re a fellow traveler, a history enthusiast, or simply someone interested in learning more about Beijing, I invite you to join me on this journey of discovery. Let’s explore the fascinating, diverse, and dynamic world together—one adventure at a time.
