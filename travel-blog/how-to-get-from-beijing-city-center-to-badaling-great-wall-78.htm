How to Get from Beijing City Center to Badaling Great Wall

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

The Badaling Great Wall is one of the most popular and well-preserved sections of the Great Wall of China. Located about 70 kilometers (43 miles) from downtown Beijing, it attracts millions of tourists each year. If you're planning a visit to this iconic landmark, knowing how to get from Beijing city center to Badaling is essential for making your trip smooth and enjoyable. Fortunately, there are several transportation options available, each suited to different preferences, budgets, and time constraints. Whether you want the convenience of a guided tour, the speed of a direct train, or the adventure of a public bus, you can easily access Badaling from Beijing’s city center.

1. Taking the Train to Badaling Great Wall

One of the most efficient and affordable ways to get from Beijing city center to Badaling is by taking the train. This option offers a comfortable and relatively quick journey, with direct connections to the Great Wall. The train ride offers an opportunity to enjoy the scenic countryside of Beijing, providing a unique experience before you even reach your destination.

How to Catch the Train

To reach Badaling from downtown Beijing, you can take the S2 train from Beijing North Railway Station (Beijing Bei Zhan). The S2 train is a dedicated service that goes directly to Badaling, making it a convenient option for visitors.

  • Location of Beijing North Railway Station: The station is located near the central business district of Beijing and can be easily reached by subway or taxi.
  • Departure Schedule: The S2 train typically runs every hour, starting from early morning and continuing until the afternoon. The exact departure times may vary, so it’s recommended to check the schedule ahead of time.
  • Ticket Price: A one-way ticket for the S2 train costs around 6-8 CNY, making it one of the most affordable options for tourists.
  • Travel Time: The train ride takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on the time of day and any potential delays.

Advantages of Taking the Train

  • Affordability: The train is a budget-friendly option for travelers, especially those who want to avoid the higher costs of taxis or private tours.
  • Convenience: The S2 train offers a direct route to Badaling, so you don’t have to worry about transfers or complicated directions.
  • Scenic Experience: The train ride offers a chance to see rural Beijing, which can be a refreshing contrast to the bustling city.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Crowded Train: The train can become crowded, especially during weekends and holidays. If you are traveling during peak times, it’s a good idea to arrive early to secure a seat.
  • Limited Service: The S2 train operates only during certain hours, so if you miss a train or are planning a late visit, you might need to consider alternative transport options.

2. Taking a Bus to Badaling

If you prefer a more affordable public transportation option, taking a bus from Beijing city center to Badaling is another popular choice. The long-distance buses that go to Badaling are operated by multiple companies, and they leave from several locations within the city.

Bus Routes to Badaling

  • Route 877: One of the most common buses used by tourists traveling to Badaling Great Wall is bus route 877. This bus departs from the Deshengmen station, which is located on the northern edge of Beijing’s city center. The ride is direct and relatively inexpensive.
  • Bus Departure Points: Other departure points for buses heading to Badaling include Xizhimen and Qianmen. Depending on your location in the city, you can choose the nearest departure point to catch the bus.
  • Cost: The cost for a one-way bus ticket is typically around 12 to 25 CNY, depending on the bus service and your departure location.
  • Travel Time: The bus journey takes around 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions and the time of day.

Advantages of Taking the Bus

  • Affordable: Taking the bus is one of the cheapest ways to get to Badaling.
  • Multiple Departure Points: There are several locations in Beijing where you can catch a bus to Badaling, providing flexibility for visitors.
  • Scenic Route: Like the train, taking the bus offers a chance to see some of the countryside as you approach the Great Wall.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Traffic: Beijing is notorious for its traffic jams, and the bus ride can take longer during peak travel times. Be prepared for potential delays, especially during rush hours.
  • Limited Comfort: The bus may not offer the same level of comfort as other options like taxis or private cars. The ride could be crowded, and space for luggage may be limited.

3. Taking a Taxi to Badaling

For those who prefer a more direct and private mode of transport, taking a taxi from Beijing city center to Badaling is a convenient option. While taxis are more expensive than public transportation, they offer the benefit of direct service, flexibility, and a more comfortable experience.

How to Book a Taxi

  • Hailing a Taxi: Taxis can be hailed on the street or booked using ride-hailing apps like Didi.
  • Cost: The cost of a taxi ride to Badaling typically ranges from 200 to 300 CNY, depending on the starting location in the city center. Additional fees for tolls or traffic delays may apply.
  • Travel Time: The drive takes approximately 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions. It’s recommended to plan your journey during off-peak hours to avoid the heavy congestion that is common in Beijing.
  • Taxi Availability: Taxis are available 24/7, so you can opt for this option if you need more flexibility regarding travel time.

Advantages of Taking a Taxi

  • Comfort: Taxis are more comfortable than public transportation, with air conditioning, ample seating, and the convenience of a private ride.
  • Direct Service: A taxi will take you directly to the entrance of the Badaling Great Wall, saving you time and effort compared to other forms of transport that may require transfers.
  • Flexibility: You can choose your departure time and adjust your schedule as needed.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Higher Cost: Taking a taxi is significantly more expensive than public transportation options, especially for budget travelers.
  • Traffic Delays: As with buses, the journey can be affected by traffic, particularly if you travel during peak hours.

4. Private Tours to Badaling

If you prefer a hassle-free, guided experience, booking a private tour to Badaling can be an excellent choice. Many tour companies in Beijing offer day trips to the Great Wall, with the Badaling section being a key stop. These tours typically include round-trip transportation, a knowledgeable guide, and sometimes additional services such as meals and entrance tickets.

Types of Private Tours

  • Group Tours: These are more affordable and often operate with a fixed schedule, where you travel with a group of people.
  • Private Customized Tours: These tours offer greater flexibility, allowing you to choose the time, duration, and pace of your visit. You’ll have a private vehicle and a guide dedicated to your group.

Cost and Travel Time

The cost of a private tour can vary depending on the type of tour you select. A standard group tour might cost around 200 to 400 CNY per person, while private tours can range from 500 to 1500 CNY or more, depending on the level of service. The travel time will generally be around 1.5 to 2 hours.

Advantages of Private Tours

  • Convenience: Everything is taken care of for you, including transportation, entrance tickets, and sometimes meals.
  • Guided Experience: With a private tour, you’ll have a knowledgeable guide who can provide interesting facts and history about the Great Wall, making your visit more informative.
  • Flexibility: Customized tours allow you to choose your preferred departure times, allowing for a more relaxed and personalized experience.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Higher Cost: Private tours can be quite expensive, especially if you opt for a customized experience.
  • Group Tour Crowds: Group tours might be crowded, particularly during peak travel seasons.

5. Driving Yourself to Badaling

For adventurous travelers who are comfortable driving in Beijing, renting a car is an option. This gives you complete freedom to explore at your own pace and allows you to avoid the crowds of public transportation.

How to Rent a Car

  • Car rental services are available at Beijing Capital International Airport and at other locations in the city. It’s recommended to book your car in advance to ensure availability.
  • Driving directions to Badaling are relatively straightforward, and navigation apps like Baidu Maps or Google Maps can provide turn-by-turn instructions.

Cost and Travel Time

The cost of renting a car can vary, but it typically ranges from 200 to 500 CNY per day, depending on the car type. The drive to Badaling takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions.

Advantages of Renting a Car

  • Flexibility: You have the freedom to stop along the way and explore the surroundings at your own pace.
  • Comfort: Renting a car allows for a private and comfortable journey, especially if you are traveling with family or a group.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Driving in Beijing: Beijing’s traffic can be challenging for first-time visitors, and parking at Badaling can be limited during peak times.
  • Tolls: There are tolls on the highways leading to Badaling, so make sure to have cash or an electronic payment method available.

Whether you choose to take the train, bus, taxi, or a private tour, there are several ways to reach Badaling Great Wall from Beijing city center. Each option offers different advantages depending on your budget, time constraints, and preferences. With careful planning, you can ensure a smooth and enjoyable journey to one of China’s most iconic landmarks.
