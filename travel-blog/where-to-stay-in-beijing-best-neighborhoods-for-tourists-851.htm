Where to Stay in Beijing: Best Neighborhoods for Tourists

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, China’s sprawling capital, is a city where ancient history collides with modern innovation. Whether you’re here to explore the Forbidden City, hike the Great Wall, or indulge in Peking duck, choosing the right neighborhood to stay in can make or break your trip. From bustling commercial hubs to quaint hutong alleys, here’s a breakdown of the best areas for tourists.

1. Dongcheng District: The Heart of Historic Beijing

Why Stay Here?

Dongcheng is home to some of Beijing’s most iconic landmarks, including the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, and the Temple of Heaven. If you’re a first-time visitor who wants to be steps away from history, this is the place.

Top Sub-Neighborhoods

  • Wangfujing: A shopper’s paradise with luxury malls, street food stalls (try the scorpion skewers!), and the famous Wangfujing Night Market.
  • Nanluoguxiang: A charming hutong area filled with boutique shops, hip cafés, and traditional courtyard homes (siheyuan).
  • Qianmen: Located just south of Tiananmen, this area offers easy access to major sights and has a revitalized pedestrian street with Qing Dynasty architecture.

Best Hotels

  • The Peninsula Beijing (Luxury)
  • Hotel Kapok (Boutique)
  • Peking International Youth Hostel (Budget)

2. Xicheng District: Culture & Scenic Views

Why Stay Here?

Xicheng is quieter than Dongcheng but equally rich in culture. It’s ideal for travelers who want a mix of sightseeing and relaxation, with attractions like Beihai Park and the Lama Temple nearby.

Top Sub-Neighborhoods

  • Shichahai: A picturesque lakeside area with bars, teahouses, and rickshaw tours through hutongs.
  • Xidan: A commercial hub with great shopping and dining options, though less touristy than Wangfujing.
  • Houhai: Known for its nightlife—think rooftop bars with views of the lake.

Best Hotels

  • The Orchid Hotel (Boutique)
  • Fairmont Beijing (Luxury)
  • 365 Inn (Budget)

3. Chaoyang District: Modern & International Vibes

Why Stay Here?

Chaoyang is Beijing’s most cosmopolitan district, packed with embassies, high-end malls, and nightlife. It’s perfect for travelers who want a mix of Western comforts and local flavor.

Top Sub-Neighborhoods

  • Sanlitun: The epicenter of Beijing’s nightlife, with clubs, cocktail bars, and the famous Taikoo Li shopping complex.
  • Guomao (CBD): Skyscrapers, luxury hotels, and the CCTV Tower dominate this financial hub.
  • 798 Art Zone: A hipster haven with galleries, cafés, and street art—great for creative souls.

Best Hotels

  • The Opposite House (Luxury)
  • Hotel G Beijing (Boutique)
  • Red Lantern House (Budget)

4. Haidian District: For Families & Academics

Why Stay Here?

Haidian is home to Peking University, Tsinghua University, and the Summer Palace. It’s greener and more relaxed, making it great for families or those who prefer a slower pace.

Top Sub-Neighborhoods

  • Wudaokou: A student-heavy area with affordable eats, lively bars, and a youthful vibe.
  • Zhongguancun: Beijing’s "Silicon Valley," with tech stores and innovation hubs.
  • Fragrant Hills: A scenic retreat, especially stunning in autumn when the leaves change color.

Best Hotels

  • InterContinental Beijing Beichen (Luxury)
  • Jia Hotel Beijing (Mid-range)
  • Beijing Far East International Youth Hostel (Budget)

5. Fengtai District: Gateway to the Great Wall

Why Stay Here?

Fengtai is less touristy but offers convenient access to the Great Wall at Badaling and the Marco Polo Bridge. It’s a practical choice for those prioritizing day trips over city exploration.

Top Sub-Neighborhoods

  • Lugouqiao (Marco Polo Bridge): A historic area with fewer crowds.
  • Fengtai Sports Center: A newer development with modern amenities.

Best Hotels

  • Pullman Beijing South (Mid-range)
  • GreenTree Inn (Budget)

Bonus: Airport Areas (Shunyi & Daxing)

If you have an early flight or a layover, staying near Beijing Capital International Airport (Shunyi) or Daxing Airport can save time.

Best Hotels

  • Novotel Beijing Peace (Near Capital Airport)
  • Hilton Beijing Daxing (Near Daxing Airport)

No matter where you stay, Beijing’s efficient subway system makes it easy to explore. Just remember: the best neighborhood depends on your travel style—whether you’re a history buff, a partygoer, or a nature lover. Happy travels!
