Winter in Beijing: A Cozy and Scenic Travel Plan

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Winter in Beijing is a magical time when the city transforms into a serene wonderland. The crisp air, snow-dusted rooftops, and festive atmosphere make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re exploring ancient temples, indulging in hotpot, or ice-skating on Houhai Lake, Beijing offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity during the colder months. Here’s your ultimate guide to experiencing the best of Beijing in winter.

Why Visit Beijing in Winter?

Many travelers overlook Beijing in winter, assuming it’s too cold or lacking in activities. However, this season has its own charm:

  • Fewer Crowds: Popular attractions like the Great Wall and Forbidden City are less crowded, allowing for a more intimate experience.
  • Stunning Scenery: Snow-covered palaces and frozen lakes create picture-perfect moments.
  • Winter Festivals: From ice lantern displays to temple fairs, Beijing’s cultural events shine in winter.
  • Cozy Culinary Delights: Warm up with steaming bowls of lamb hotpot or sweet roasted sweet potatoes from street vendors.

Top Winter Attractions in Beijing

The Great Wall in Snow

Few sights rival the Great Wall draped in snow. The Mutianyu and Badaling sections are particularly breathtaking, with their watchtowers dusted in white. For an unforgettable experience, hike early in the morning when the crowds are thin and the sunlight glistens on the frost.

Pro Tip: Wear sturdy boots with good traction—some sections can be slippery!

Forbidden City & Jingshan Park

The Forbidden City takes on a mystical aura in winter, with its golden roofs contrasting against the snow. After exploring the palace, climb Jingshan Park for a panoramic view of the entire complex blanketed in white.

Houhai Lake Ice Skating

Houhai Lake turns into a giant ice rink in winter, where locals and tourists glide across the frozen surface. Rent skates or try a traditional ice bike for a fun, nostalgic activity. Nearby, cozy cafés and bars offer hot drinks to warm up afterward.

Temple of Heaven Snowscape

The Temple of Heaven’s vast courtyards and iconic Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests look ethereal under a layer of snow. Visit at sunrise for a peaceful stroll and stunning photo opportunities.

Longqing Gorge Ice Festival

A short trip from Beijing, the Longqing Gorge Ice Festival features massive ice sculptures, illuminated tunnels, and frozen waterfalls. It’s a dazzling display of artistry and winter magic.

Cozy Winter Eats & Drinks

Beijing’s winter cuisine is all about warmth and comfort. Here are must-try dishes:

Lamb Hotpot (Shuàn Yángròu)

Nothing beats gathering around a bubbling pot of lamb hotpot on a chilly day. Thinly sliced lamb, fresh veggies, and rich broth make this a quintessential Beijing winter meal.

Roasted Sweet Potatoes & Chestnuts

Street vendors roast sweet potatoes and chestnuts over charcoal, filling the air with a smoky-sweet aroma. These cheap and delicious snacks are perfect for warming your hands while exploring.

Warm Soy Milk & Jianbing

Start your morning with a cup of hot soy milk and jianbing (savory Chinese crepes). These breakfast staples are hearty and satisfying.

Peking Duck with a Winter Twist

Peking Duck is a year-round favorite, but winter adds extra coziness. Pair it with scallions, pancakes, and a dash of hoisin sauce for the ultimate comfort meal.

Unique Winter Experiences

Hutong Walks at Dusk

Beijing’s ancient hutongs (narrow alleyways) are especially charming in winter. Wander through these historic neighborhoods as lanterns glow and smoke curls from chimney stacks. Stop by a local tea house for a warming cup of pu’er tea.

Skiing Near Beijing

For adventure seekers, Nanshan Ski Village and Huaibei International Ski Resort offer slopes just a short drive from the city. Whether you’re a beginner or expert, skiing here is a great way to embrace the season.

New Year Temple Fairs

If visiting around Chinese New Year, don’t miss the temple fairs at Ditan Park or Longtan Park. These lively events feature traditional performances, handicrafts, and street food.

Practical Tips for Winter Travel

  • Dress in Layers: Temperatures can drop below freezing, so thermal wear, a down jacket, and waterproof boots are essential.
  • Check Attraction Hours: Some sites close earlier in winter, so plan accordingly.
  • Use Ride-Hailing Apps: Didi (China’s Uber) is a convenient way to get around without braving the cold.
  • Stay Hydrated: Cold air can be dehydrating—carry a thermos with warm water or tea.

Where to Stay: Cozy Accommodations

Luxury: The Opposite House

This stylish boutique hotel in Sanlitun offers sleek design and a spa—perfect for unwinding after a day in the cold.

Mid-Range: Courtyard 7

A beautifully restored hutong guesthouse with heated floors and traditional décor, blending comfort and culture.

Budget: Peking International Youth Hostel

A friendly, centrally located hostel with a warm common area—ideal for meeting fellow travelers.

Winter in Beijing is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its mix of scenic beauty, cultural richness, and hearty cuisine, it’s a season that promises unforgettable memories. Pack your warmest clothes and get ready to fall in love with Beijing’s winter charm!
