Things to Keep in Mind When Visiting Yonghe Gong (Lama Temple)

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Yonghe Gong, also known as the Lama Temple, is one of Beijing’s most iconic religious and cultural landmarks. Located in the city’s Dongcheng District, this Tibetan Buddhist temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, serene atmosphere, and deep spiritual significance. Visiting Yonghe Gong offers an opportunity to explore a blend of Han Chinese and Tibetan cultural elements, as well as to witness the devout practices of Buddhist worshippers. However, like many sacred sites, it is essential to approach your visit with respect and preparation. Here are the key things you should know and consider when planning your trip to Yonghe Gong.

1. Understand the Cultural and Religious Significance

Yonghe Gong is not just a tourist attraction—it is an active place of worship. Originally built in the 17th century as a residence for Emperor Yongzheng before being converted into a lamasery in 1744, the temple is now one of the most important Tibetan Buddhist temples outside of Tibet. The site houses numerous halls, prayer wheels, and a towering statue of Maitreya Buddha, making it a spiritual haven for both locals and visitors.

To fully appreciate your visit, take some time to learn about Tibetan Buddhism and the temple’s history. Understanding the rituals and practices observed here will enhance your experience and help you respect the cultural and spiritual essence of the temple.

2. Plan Your Visit Wisely

Yonghe Gong is a popular attraction, and its central location makes it easily accessible to both locals and tourists. To avoid crowds, it’s best to visit early in the morning or on weekdays. During major Buddhist festivals, the temple can become particularly busy, as worshippers flock to participate in ceremonies.

The temple opens daily from 9:00 AM to 4:30 PM, but hours may vary during holidays. Allocate at least two hours for your visit to explore the various halls, admire the intricate architecture, and soak in the serene ambiance.

3. Dress Modestly and Respectfully

Since Yonghe Gong is a sacred site, it is important to dress appropriately. Avoid wearing revealing clothing such as shorts, tank tops, or crop tops. Opt for modest attire that covers your shoulders and knees. Dressing respectfully not only shows consideration for the religious significance of the site but also ensures you feel comfortable during your visit.

4. Purchase Tickets in Advance

To enter Yonghe Gong, visitors must purchase tickets. As of recent years, the entrance fee is typically around 25 RMB per person. Tickets can be purchased at the temple gate or online through authorized platforms. Booking online is recommended, especially during peak seasons, as it helps avoid long queues and guarantees your entry.

Keep your ticket with you throughout your visit, as you may need to show it at various checkpoints within the temple complex.

5. Photography Rules

Photography is allowed in certain areas of the temple, but it’s prohibited inside the halls and sacred spaces. Flash photography and the use of tripods are also generally discouraged, as they can disrupt the tranquil atmosphere and the worshippers’ experience. Look for signs indicating whether photography is permitted and always follow the rules.

If you’re unsure, it’s best to ask a staff member or observe what others are doing. Capturing the stunning exteriors and courtyards of the temple is often sufficient to document your visit.

6. Respect Worshippers and Rituals

Yonghe Gong remains an active place of worship, and you are likely to encounter devotees performing rituals, lighting incense, or making offerings. Be mindful of their practices and avoid interrupting or photographing them.

If you wish to participate in lighting incense, do so respectfully. Incense sticks are available for purchase at the temple entrance, and they should be placed in designated burners only. Avoid excessive smoke or discarding incense in inappropriate areas.

7. Explore the Key Halls and Structures

The Yonghe Gong complex comprises several significant halls, each with unique architectural and spiritual elements. Here are some highlights to include in your visit:

  • Hall of Heavenly Kings (Tianwang Dian): This is the first hall you will encounter, featuring statues of the Four Heavenly Kings and Maitreya Buddha.
  • Hall of Harmony and Peace (Yonghe Dian): The main hall houses three bronze Buddhas representing the past, present, and future.
  • Hall of Everlasting Protection (Falun Dian): Known for its massive prayer wheel and an 18-meter statue of Tsongkhapa, the founder of the Gelug school of Tibetan Buddhism.
  • Pavilion of Ten Thousand Happinesses (Wanfuge): This pavilion contains an awe-inspiring 26-meter-tall statue of Maitreya Buddha carved from a single piece of white sandalwood.

Take your time to admire the intricate details of the statues, paintings, and carvings in each hall.

8. Refrain From Touching Sacred Objects

Many of the artifacts and statues within Yonghe Gong are centuries old and hold deep spiritual significance. Avoid touching these sacred objects, as oils from your skin can damage them over time. Respect the sanctity of the site by observing and appreciating these treasures from a respectful distance.

9. Follow Temple Etiquette

When walking through the temple, follow these basic rules of etiquette to ensure a harmonious visit:

  • Always move clockwise around the halls and prayer wheels, as this is the traditional Buddhist practice.
  • Speak softly to maintain the peaceful atmosphere.
  • Do not point your feet at statues or altars, as this is considered disrespectful.
  • Avoid stepping over thresholds when entering halls; step over them instead.

10. Take Public Transportation

Yonghe Gong is conveniently located near major public transportation hubs, making it easy to reach without the hassle of driving or parking. The Yonghegong Lama Temple metro station serves both Line 2 and Line 5, and it’s just a short walk from the temple entrance.

If you prefer to take a taxi, make sure to show the driver the name "雍和宫" (Yonghe Gong) to avoid confusion.

11. Stay Hydrated and Comfortable

Beijing’s weather can vary greatly depending on the season. During summer, temperatures can soar, so bring water and wear a hat to stay cool. In winter, the cold can be biting, so dress warmly. Comfortable footwear is a must, as you’ll be walking on stone pathways throughout the temple complex.

12. Visit Nearby Attractions

After exploring Yonghe Gong, consider visiting nearby attractions to complete your day. Confucius Temple and Guozijian Street are within walking distance and offer a glimpse into China’s rich cultural and educational history. These sites provide a great complement to your visit, giving you a broader understanding of Beijing’s heritage.

Yonghe Gong is more than just a tourist destination; it is a place of deep cultural and spiritual resonance. By following these tips and respecting the temple’s traditions, you can ensure a meaningful and memorable visit to one of Beijing’s most cherished landmarks.
