How to Spend 5 Days in Beijing: A Detailed Travel Plan

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, China’s capital, is a city where ancient history and modernity collide. From the iconic Great Wall to the bustling hutongs, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. If you’ve got five days to explore, here’s a detailed itinerary to help you make the most of your trip.

Day 1: Imperial Beijing

Morning: The Forbidden City

Start your adventure at the Forbidden City, the world’s largest imperial palace complex. Spend at least 3 hours exploring its grand halls, intricate courtyards, and hidden gems like the Clock Exhibition Hall. Don’t forget to climb Jingshan Park afterward for a panoramic view of the palace.

Afternoon: Tiananmen Square & Wangfujing

A short walk south leads you to Tiananmen Square, one of the largest public squares in the world. Snap photos of the Monument to the People’s Heroes and Mao Zedong’s Mausoleum. Then, head to Wangfujing Street for lunch—try local snacks like jianbing (savory crepes) or peking duck at Quanjude.

Evening: Hutong Food Tour

Wrap up your day with a hutong food tour. These narrow alleyways are packed with hidden eateries serving lamb skewers, zhajiangmian (noodles with soybean paste), and stinky tofu (if you’re brave).

Day 2: The Great Wall Adventure

Morning: Mutianyu Great Wall

Skip the crowded Badaling and head to Mutianyu, a quieter but equally stunning section. Take the cable car up and hike along the wall for breathtaking views. The toboggan ride down is a must for thrill-seekers!

Afternoon: Lunch in a Local Village

Stop by a nearby village for a rustic lunch—think farm-to-table dishes like braised pork and wild mushrooms.

Evening: Sanlitun Nightlife

Back in the city, unwind in Sanlitun, Beijing’s trendy district. Sip craft cocktails at The Bell & Drum or dance the night away at One Third.

Day 3: Temples & Parks

Morning: Temple of Heaven

Visit the Temple of Heaven, where emperors once prayed for good harvests. Join locals practicing tai chi or playing jianzi (hacky sack) in the park.

Afternoon: Summer Palace

Spend the afternoon at the Summer Palace, a UNESCO-listed retreat with a massive lake and ornate pavilions. Rent a paddleboat or stroll along the Long Corridor, adorned with thousands of paintings.

Evening: Kung Fu Show

Catch a Legend of Kung Fu Show at the Red Theatre—a dazzling mix of martial arts and storytelling.

Day 4: Art & Modern Beijing

Morning: 798 Art District

Explore 798 Art Zone, a hipster paradise filled with galleries, street art, and quirky cafes. Don’t miss the UCCA Center for Contemporary Art.

Afternoon: Olympic Park

See the Bird’s Nest and Water Cube from the 2008 Olympics. If you’re visiting in winter, the Water Cube transforms into an ice park.

Evening: Ghost Street (Gui Jie)

Dine on spicy crayfish or hot pot on Gui Jie, a street famous for its neon lights and late-night eats.

Day 5: Day Trip & Souvenirs

Morning: Lama Temple & Confucius Temple

Visit the Yonghe Lama Temple, Beijing’s largest Tibetan Buddhist temple, then walk to the serene Confucius Temple.

Afternoon: Panjiayuan Market

Hunt for souvenirs at Panjiayuan Antique Market—everything from calligraphy scrolls to Mao-era memorabilia. Bargaining is expected!

Evening: Rooftop Bar Farewell

End your trip with cocktails at Migas Mercado, a rooftop bar with skyline views.

This itinerary balances history, culture, and fun—ensuring you experience the best of Beijing in just five days!
