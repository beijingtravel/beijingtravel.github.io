Fall Foliage Road Trips from Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

As autumn paints the landscapes in fiery hues of red, orange, and gold, there’s no better time to hit the road from Beijing and witness nature’s spectacular show. Whether you’re a photography enthusiast, a nature lover, or simply seeking a weekend escape, these fall foliage road trips offer breathtaking views, cultural gems, and unforgettable adventures.

The Best Time to Go

Timing is everything when it comes to fall foliage. Near Beijing, peak colors typically appear from mid-October to early November. Higher elevations like those in Hebei province change earlier, while lower areas near the city may hold their colors a bit longer. Keep an eye on local foliage reports to catch the most vibrant displays.

Top Fall Foliage Road Trips

1. Fragrant Hills (Xiangshan)

Distance from Beijing: ~20 km (less than 1 hour drive)

One of the most accessible spots for autumn colors, Fragrant Hills transforms into a sea of red maple leaves. While it’s a popular destination, arriving early in the morning helps avoid crowds. The Smoking Tree (Hongye) area is particularly stunning, with winding trails leading to panoramic views.

Pro Tip: Pair your visit with a stop at the Beijing Botanical Garden, where fall blooms and golden ginkgo trees add to the seasonal charm.

2. Bashang Grasslands & Saihanba National Forest Park

Distance from Beijing: ~300 km (4-5 hours drive)

For a more remote and dramatic fall experience, head north to the Bashang Grasslands and Saihanba National Forest Park. The mix of grasslands, birch forests, and rolling hills creates a surreal golden landscape. This route is perfect for those who love open roads and fewer tourists.

Don’t Miss: The Moon Lake (Yueya Quan) area, where the reflection of autumn colors on the water makes for incredible photos.

3. Chengde & the Imperial Mountain Resort

Distance from Beijing: ~230 km (3-4 hours drive)

A blend of history and nature, Chengde’s Imperial Mountain Resort (Bishu Shanzhuang) is surrounded by forests that turn brilliant shades of yellow and red in fall. The resort itself, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, offers serene lakes and pavilions framed by autumn foliage.

Side Trip: Drive further to Jinshanling Great Wall, where the ancient ruins are draped in fall colors—a perfect combo of history and natural beauty.

4. Miyun Reservoir & Gubei Water Town

Distance from Beijing: ~120 km (2 hours drive)

For a lakeside autumn escape, Miyun Reservoir offers peaceful drives with water views flanked by golden trees. Nearby, Gubei Water Town mimics a traditional Jiangnan water village, with willow trees and stone bridges enhanced by fall hues. At night, the town lights up, creating a magical atmosphere.

Bonus: Take the Simatai Great Wall cable car for an aerial view of the surrounding autumn scenery.

5. Red Leaf Valley (Hongye Gu) in Baoding

Distance from Beijing: ~200 km (3 hours drive)

True to its name, Red Leaf Valley is one of North China’s best-kept secrets for fall foliage. The valley is packed with wild maple trees that turn a deep crimson, creating a striking contrast against the rocky cliffs. Hiking trails range from easy walks to more challenging routes.

Local Flavor: Try Hebei’s rustic cuisine, like iron pot stew (tieguo dun), at nearby village restaurants.

Road Trip Essentials

Packing for the Journey

  • Layered clothing – Mornings and evenings can be chilly, while afternoons are mild.
  • Camera gear – A polarizing filter can enhance fall colors in photos.
  • Snacks & hydration – Stock up on nuts, fruits, and water for long drives.
  • Navigation tools – While GPS works well, offline maps are handy in remote areas.

Scenic Driving Tips

  • Weekday trips are less crowded than weekends.
  • Sunrise/sunset drives offer the best lighting for foliage.
  • Pull over safely for photos—many rural roads lack proper shoulders.

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Stops

The Secret Ginkgo Avenue in Daxing

Few know about this 6 km stretch in Beijing’s Daxing District, lined with towering ginkgo trees that turn a radiant yellow in late October. It’s a quieter alternative to the famous Diaoyutai Ginkgo Boulevard.

Ling Mountain (Lingshan)

Beijing’s highest peak, Ling Mountain, offers alpine meadows and birch forests that turn gold early in the season. The drive up is steep but rewards with sweeping views.

Yanqi Lake in Huairou

A tranquil spot near Mutianyu Great Wall, Yanqi Lake’s shoreline is fringed with poplars and maples. Rent a bike or kayak to explore the area.

Capturing the Perfect Fall Shot

Best Photography Spots

  • Mutianyu Great Wall – The contrast of ancient walls and autumn leaves is iconic.
  • Gubei Water Town’s canals – Reflections of red leaves on water are dreamy.
  • Bashang’s lone trees – Perfect for minimalist landscape shots.

Golden Hour Magic

The hour after sunrise and before sunset casts a warm glow on foliage, making colors pop. Use a telephoto lens to compress layers of trees for a painterly effect.

Local Eats Along the Way

Must-Try Autumn Dishes

  • Roasted sweet potatoes – Sold by street vendors, a classic fall snack.
  • Persimmon cakes (shibing) – A seasonal treat in rural Hebei.
  • Hot pot – Warm up with a hearty meal after a day of leaf-peeping.

Cozy Cafés & Tea Houses

  • The Orchard (near Xiangshan) – A rustic café with apple-picking in season.
  • Gubei Water Town’s rooftop tea houses – Sip tea while overlooking autumn vistas.

Sustainable Travel Tips

  • Stay on marked trails to protect fragile ecosystems.
  • Support local businesses by buying handicrafts or dining at family-run restaurants.
  • Carry a trash bag—many scenic areas lack proper waste bins.

With crisp air, dazzling colors, and open roads, fall is the ultimate season for a Beijing getaway. Whether you chase the iconic red maples of Xiangshan or venture into the wild grasslands of Bashang, these road trips promise an unforgettable autumn escape.
