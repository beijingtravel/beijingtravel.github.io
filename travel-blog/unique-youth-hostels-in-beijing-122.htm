Unique Youth Hostels in Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Beijing, China's capital, is not only rich in history and culture but also offers diverse accommodation options for travelers. Among these, youth hostels stand out for their affordability, vibrant atmosphere, and often unique designs. Whether you're a solo backpacker or a group of friends exploring the city, staying in a youth hostel can enhance your travel experience. Here are some of the most distinctive youth hostels in Beijing.

Peking Yard Hostel

Peking Yard Hostel is a gem located in the heart of Beijing, near the Zhangzizhonglu subway station. It is housed in a traditional siheyuan, or courtyard house, offering guests an authentic experience of Beijing's old-world charm.

Highlights

  • Authentic Design: The hostel features beautiful courtyards adorned with flowers and traditional Chinese decor.
  • Convenience: Its central location makes it easy to access major attractions such as the Forbidden City and Tiananmen Square.
  • Community Vibe: The communal areas encourage interaction among travelers, making it a great place to meet people.

Price Range

Dormitory beds typically cost around 100 RMB per night, while private rooms range from 300 to 500 RMB.

Beijing Downtown Backpackers Hostel

Located in Nanluoguxiang, one of Beijingâ€™s most famous hutongs, this hostel provides a mix of cultural immersion and modern comfort. Its proximity to trendy shops, cafes, and historical sites makes it a favorite among young travelers.

Highlights

  • Prime Location: Nanluoguxiang is a bustling area filled with street food, artisan shops, and traditional architecture.
  • Tours and Activities: The hostel organizes tours to the Great Wall, bike rentals, and cultural workshops.
  • Facilities: It offers free Wi-Fi, a shared kitchen, and a cozy common room.

Price Range

Dormitory beds are available for about 120 RMB per night, and private rooms range from 350 to 600 RMB.

365 Inn

365 Inn is situated near Qianmen, a historic district close to many of Beijing's iconic landmarks. This hostel is ideal for those who want to be within walking distance of attractions like the Temple of Heaven and Tiananmen Square.

Highlights

  • Modern Amenities: The hostel combines contemporary design with a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Bar and Restaurant: A lively bar serves drinks and meals, making it easy to unwind after a day of sightseeing.
  • Central Location: Its proximity to public transport makes exploring Beijing convenient.

Price Range

Beds in shared dormitories cost around 80-100 RMB per night, and private rooms are available from 300 RMB.

Happy Dragon Alley Hostel

Located near the Dongsi subway station, Happy Dragon Alley Hostel offers an excellent blend of affordability and convenience. Its welcoming environment and helpful staff make it a popular choice for international travelers.

Highlights

  • Friendly Atmosphere: The staff is known for their hospitality and can help arrange tours and tickets.
  • Comfortable Rooms: Both dormitories and private rooms are clean and well-maintained.
  • On-Site Bar: The Dragon Bar is a great spot to relax and mingle with other guests.

Price Range

Dormitory beds start at 90 RMB per night, and private rooms cost between 250 and 400 RMB.

The Great Wall Box House

For travelers seeking a unique experience, The Great Wall Box House is located in the Gubeikou area, providing easy access to less-touristy sections of the Great Wall.

Highlights

  • Proximity to the Great Wall: The hostel is a short walk from Gubeikou, a quieter and more scenic section of the wall.
  • Rustic Charm: Its minimalist design and natural surroundings offer a peaceful retreat from the bustling city.
  • Activities: Guests can participate in hiking tours and enjoy stunning sunrise views over the Great Wall.

Price Range

Dorm beds are available for around 150 RMB, and private rooms range from 400 to 600 RMB.

Drum Tower Youth Hostel

Located near the iconic Drum and Bell Towers, this hostel combines modern comfort with a traditional setting. The area is lively, with many restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions nearby.

Highlights

  • Central Location: Close to Houhai, a popular spot for nightlife and dining.
  • Terrace Views: The rooftop terrace offers fantastic views of the Drum and Bell Towers.
  • Friendly Staff: Staff members are helpful and can assist with planning your Beijing itinerary.

Price Range

Dormitory beds start at 90 RMB, and private rooms are available for 300-500 RMB.

Red Lantern House

Red Lantern House is another courtyard-style hostel located in a quiet hutong. It offers an authentic Beijing experience while providing all the necessary modern amenities.

Highlights

  • Traditional Atmosphere: The hostelâ€™s decor and layout immerse you in Beijingâ€™s cultural heritage.
  • Family-Run: Guests often praise the warm and welcoming staff.
  • Convenience: Easy access to major attractions like the Forbidden City and Beihai Park.

Price Range

Dorm beds cost around 100 RMB, while private rooms are priced between 350 and 500 RMB.

Tips for Choosing a Youth Hostel in Beijing

Location Matters

Beijing is vast, so choose a hostel close to the areas you plan to explore. Hostels in hutongs or near subway lines offer a great balance of accessibility and cultural immersion.

Consider Your Budget

While youth hostels are generally affordable, prices can vary depending on location, amenities, and room type. Book early to secure the best deals.

Look for Reviews

Reading reviews on platforms like Hostelworld or Booking.com can help you gauge the quality and atmosphere of a hostel.

Check for Activities

Many hostels in Beijing organize tours, cultural workshops, and social events. These can enhance your experience and help you make new friends.

Prepare for the Language Barrier

While many hostel staff speak English, itâ€™s helpful to have translation apps or learn a few basic Mandarin phrases.
