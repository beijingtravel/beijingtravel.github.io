How to Visit the Lama Temple in Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing is a city where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern life, and one of its most fascinating spiritual landmarks is the Lama Temple (Yonghegong). This Tibetan Buddhist monastery is not only a place of worship but also a cultural treasure trove that attracts travelers from around the world. Whether you're a history buff, a spiritual seeker, or simply a curious explorer, visiting the Lama Temple is an unforgettable experience. Here’s everything you need to know to make the most of your visit.

Why the Lama Temple Should Be on Your Beijing Itinerary

The Lama Temple, officially known as Yonghegong (Palace of Peace and Harmony), is one of the most important Tibetan Buddhist monasteries outside of Tibet. Built in 1694 during the Qing Dynasty, it originally served as the residence of Emperor Yongzheng before being converted into a lamasery in 1744. Today, it’s a living temple where monks practice daily rituals, making it a rare glimpse into authentic Tibetan Buddhist traditions in the heart of Beijing.

Key Highlights of the Lama Temple

  • The Five Great Halls: The temple complex consists of five main halls, each with its own significance. The Hall of the Heavenly Kings, the Hall of Harmony and Peace, and the Pavilion of Ten Thousand Happinesses are must-sees.
  • The 18-Meter Maitreya Buddha: Inside the Wanfu Pavilion stands a towering sandalwood statue of Maitreya, the future Buddha, carved from a single tree. It’s a Guinness World Record holder and a breathtaking sight.
  • Incense Offerings: Visitors can participate in the traditional practice of burning incense, believed to bring blessings and purify the mind.

Planning Your Visit: Practical Tips

Best Time to Go

The Lama Temple is open year-round, but the best times to visit are early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds. Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer pleasant weather, while winter provides a serene, less touristy experience. Avoid major Buddhist holidays if you prefer a quieter atmosphere.

How to Get There

Located in Beijing’s Dongcheng District, the temple is easily accessible via public transport:
- By Subway: Take Line 2 or Line 5 to Yonghegong Station (Exit C or D). The temple is just a short walk away.
- By Taxi or Ride-Hailing: Simply tell the driver "Yonghegong" (pronounced "Yong-huh-gong").

Tickets and Opening Hours

  • Entrance Fee: Around 25 RMB (subject to change). Cash and mobile payments (WeChat Pay/Alipay) are accepted.
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 4:30 PM (last entry at 4:00 PM).

What to Do Inside the Lama Temple

Explore the Architecture and Art

The temple’s architecture is a stunning mix of Han Chinese and Tibetan styles. Look for intricate carvings, colorful murals, and golden rooftops adorned with symbolic motifs. Don’t miss the Tibetan prayer wheels—spinning them is believed to accumulate merit.

Participate in Rituals (Respectfully)

If you visit during prayer times, you might witness monks chanting or performing ceremonies. Photography is allowed in most areas, but avoid using flash or disturbing worshippers.

Light Incense and Make a Wish

At the entrance, you can purchase incense sticks (usually 10-20 RMB). Follow the locals’ lead: light three sticks, bow three times, and place them in the incense burner while making a silent wish.

Nearby Attractions to Combine with Your Visit

Since the Lama Temple is centrally located, consider pairing your visit with these nearby spots:

Confucius Temple and Guozijian

Just a 5-minute walk away, these historic sites offer a deeper dive into China’s scholarly traditions. The quiet courtyards and ancient steles provide a peaceful contrast to the bustling city.

Hutongs Exploration

Wander through the narrow alleyways (hutongs) surrounding the temple. Stop by a traditional teahouse or try local snacks like zhajiangmian (noodles with soybean paste) at a nearby eatery.

Shopping at Wudaoying Hutong

This trendy hutong is lined with boutique shops, cafés, and souvenir stores. It’s perfect for picking up unique gifts or enjoying a post-temple coffee.

Cultural Etiquette: Do’s and Don’ts

To ensure a respectful visit, keep these tips in mind:

Do’s

  • Dress modestly (avoid shorts or sleeveless tops).
  • Walk clockwise around stupas and prayer wheels (a Tibetan Buddhist tradition).
  • Observe silence in prayer halls.

Don’ts

  • Don’t point at Buddha statues or monks.
  • Avoid touching religious artifacts.
  • Refrain from loud conversations or laughter in sacred spaces.

Insider Tips for a Memorable Experience

  • Hire a Guide or Use an Audio Tour: To fully appreciate the temple’s history, consider renting an audio guide or joining a guided tour.
  • Visit the Temple Fair (If Timing Allows): During Lunar New Year, the temple hosts vibrant festivities with lion dances and traditional performances.
  • Try Tibetan Butter Tea Nearby: Some nearby cafés serve authentic Tibetan drinks and snacks—perfect for a post-visit refreshment.

The Lama Temple is more than just a tourist attraction; it’s a window into Beijing’s spiritual soul. Whether you’re drawn to its artistry, history, or tranquil ambiance, a visit here promises to be a highlight of your Beijing adventure.
