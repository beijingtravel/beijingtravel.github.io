How to Get to Beijing’s Temples Using Public Transport

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, a city where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern life, is home to some of the most iconic temples in China. Whether you’re a history buff, a spiritual seeker, or just a curious traveler, visiting these temples is a must. But navigating Beijing’s vast public transport system can be daunting. Fear not—this guide will help you reach Beijing’s top temples efficiently and affordably.

The Lama Temple (Yonghegong)

One of Beijing’s most famous Tibetan Buddhist temples, the Lama Temple, is a stunning architectural masterpiece. Here’s how to get there:

By Subway

The easiest way is to take Subway Line 2 or Line 5 to Yonghegong Station. Exit via Exit C (Southwest Exit), and you’ll see the temple’s striking red walls just a short walk away.

By Bus

Several buses stop near the Lama Temple, including:
- Bus 13, 116, or 117 to Yonghegong Stop
- Bus 684 to Guozijian Stop, followed by a 5-minute walk

Pro tip: Arrive early to avoid crowds, especially during festivals.

The Temple of Heaven (Tiantan)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Temple of Heaven is where emperors once prayed for good harvests. It’s a sprawling complex, so plan for at least half a day here.

By Subway

Take Subway Line 5 to Tiantandongmen Station (East Gate of Temple of Heaven). From there, it’s a 5-minute walk to the entrance.

By Bus

  • Bus 36, 53, or 122 to Tiantan Beimen (North Gate)
  • Bus 120 or 2 to Tiantan Nanmen (South Gate)

Insider advice: The South Gate is less crowded and offers a grand entrance view.

The Confucius Temple and Imperial College

Located near the Lama Temple, this serene complex was once China’s highest educational institution.

By Subway

Again, Subway Line 2 or 5 to Yonghegong Station, then a 10-minute walk south along Guozijian Street.

By Bus

  • Bus 104 or 108 to Andingmen Stop, followed by a short stroll

Don’t miss: The stone tablets inscribed with the names of ancient scholars.

The White Cloud Temple (Baiyun Guan)

Beijing’s most important Taoist temple is a hidden gem with fewer tourists.

By Subway

Take Subway Line 1 to Muxidi Station, then transfer to Bus 26 or 80 to Baiyunguan Stop.

By Bus

  • Bus 26, 80, or 717 directly to Baiyunguan

Local secret: Visit during the Lunar New Year for lively temple fairs.

The Tanzhe Temple

One of Beijing’s oldest Buddhist temples, Tanzhe is nestled in the western hills. It’s a bit farther but worth the trip.

By Subway + Bus

Take Subway Line 1 to Pingguoyuan Station, then hop on Bus 931 directly to Tanzhe Temple.

By Tour Bus

During peak seasons, special tourist buses run from Qianmen to Tanzhe Temple.

Travel hack: Combine your visit with nearby Jietai Temple for a full day of temple-hopping.

General Tips for Temple Visits

  • Transport Cards: Get a Yikatong (Beijing’s transit card) for seamless travel on subways and buses.
  • Peak Hours: Avoid rush hours (7:30-9:30 AM & 5-7 PM) for a more comfortable ride.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly and follow temple rules—no loud noises or inappropriate photos.
  • Combination Tickets: Some temples offer joint tickets with nearby attractions.

Beijing’s temples are not just religious sites but windows into China’s rich cultural past. With this public transport guide, you’re ready to explore them like a local. Happy temple-hopping!
