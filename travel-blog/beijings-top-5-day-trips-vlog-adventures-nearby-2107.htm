Beijing’s Top 5 Day Trips – Vlog Adventures Nearby

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing is a city of endless possibilities, but sometimes you need to escape the hustle and bustle to explore the stunning landscapes and cultural gems just beyond the capital. Whether you're a vlogger looking for breathtaking backdrops or a traveler seeking unforgettable day trips, these five destinations offer the perfect mix of adventure, history, and natural beauty.

1. The Great Wall at Mutianyu – A Vlogger’s Dream

Why It’s a Must-Visit

The Mutianyu section of the Great Wall is one of the most picturesque and less crowded spots, making it ideal for vloggers. Unlike the tourist-heavy Badaling, Mutianyu offers sweeping views, lush greenery, and a cable car or toboggan ride for added excitement.

What to Capture

  • The Cable Car Ride: Film the ascent for stunning aerial shots of the wall snaking through the mountains.
  • The Watchtowers: Each tower has unique architecture—perfect for historical storytelling.
  • The Toboggan Slide: A fun, fast-paced descent that adds action to your vlog.

Pro Tip

Arrive early to avoid crowds and catch the golden hour light for cinematic footage.

2. Cuandixia Village – Step Back in Time

A Hidden Ancient Village

Nestled in the mountains west of Beijing, Cuandixia is a 500-year-old Ming Dynasty village frozen in time. Its stone houses, narrow alleyways, and traditional courtyards are a vlogger’s paradise for cultural content.

Highlights for Your Vlog

  • Authentic Rural Life: Interview locals making handmade crafts or farming.
  • Panoramic Views: Hike to the village’s highest point for a drone-worthy shot.
  • Local Cuisine: Film a meal of rustic dishes like jianbing (savory pancakes) and free-range chicken.

Best Time to Visit

Autumn, when the surrounding hills are painted in red and gold.

3. Shilinxia (Stone Forest Gorge) – For the Thrill-Seekers

Home to the World’s Longest Glass Platform

If your vlog thrives on adrenaline, Shilinxia’s glass walkway—jutting out over a 1,180-foot drop—will deliver heart-pounding content.

Must-Film Moments

  • The Glass Bridge Walk: Capture your reactions (or others’) as they step onto the transparent path.
  • Waterfalls and Canyons: The gorge’s natural beauty is a serene contrast to the bridge’s thrill.
  • Ziplining: Add an action sequence with the park’s zipline option.

Safety Note

The platform is sturdy, but those with acrophobia might want to skip this one!

4. Tanzhe Temple – Beijing’s Oldest Buddhist Sanctuary

A Spiritual Retreat

Dating back 1,700 years, Tanzhe Temple is older than Beijing itself. Its tranquil gardens, ancient pagodas, and incense-filled halls offer a peaceful escape from the city.

Vlog-Worthy Angles

  • The Giant Ginkgo Tree: A 1,000-year-old tree that turns brilliant yellow in fall.
  • Monk Chants: If permitted, record the hypnotic sounds of morning prayers.
  • Architecture Details: Close-ups of intricate carvings and rooftop ornaments.

Cultural Insight

Visit during a festival (like Buddha’s Birthday) for vibrant ceremonies.

5. Yanqi Lake – A Lakeside Escape

Beijing’s “Little Switzerland”

Just 60 km from the city, Yanqi Lake blends luxury and nature, with resorts, water sports, and cycling trails against a backdrop of the Yan Mountains.

Content Ideas

  • Sunrise Kayaking: Paddle across the mirror-like water at dawn.
  • Lakeside Picnic: Style a gourmet spread with the mountains as your backdrop.
  • APEC Summit Site: The modern conference center offers sleek architecture shots.

Bonus

Rent a bike and vlog the scenic lakeside loop—it’s a crowd-pleaser.

Each of these day trips unlocks a different side of Beijing’s surroundings, whether you’re chasing thrills, culture, or serenity. Pack your camera, hit record, and let these adventures fuel your next viral vlog!
