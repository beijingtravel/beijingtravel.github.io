What to Explore Inside Beijing Olympic Park

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Beijing Olympic Park, located in the northern part of Beijing, is a sprawling complex that was the central hub for the 2008 Summer Olympics and the 2022 Winter Olympics. This iconic site showcases architectural marvels, cultural experiences, and recreational areas, making it a must-visit destination for tourists and locals alike. Spanning over 11.59 square kilometers, the park combines modern sports facilities with open green spaces, offering something for everyone.

The Landmark Attractions

The Bird's Nest (National Stadium)

The Bird’s Nest, officially known as the National Stadium, is one of the most recognizable symbols of the Beijing Olympics. Its striking design, resembling a bird's nest, was created by architects Herzog & de Meuron.

  • Highlights:
    Visitors can tour the stadium’s interior to see the massive seating areas and learn about its construction and use during the Olympics. At night, the structure is illuminated, creating a magical atmosphere perfect for photos.
  • Activities:
    In addition to guided tours, the Bird's Nest occasionally hosts events, concerts, and exhibitions. During the winter, it transforms into a snow park where visitors can enjoy activities like skiing and snowboarding.

The Water Cube (National Aquatics Center)

Another architectural gem, the Water Cube, is famous for its bubble-like exterior. It was the venue for swimming, diving, and other aquatic events during the 2008 Olympics.

  • Post-Olympic Transformation:
    After the games, part of the facility was converted into a water park called Happy Magic Water Cube. This indoor park features water slides, wave pools, and play areas, making it a great spot for families.
  • Evening Beauty:
    The Water Cube lights up in vibrant colors at night, creating a stunning visual display that complements the Bird’s Nest.

Olympic Forest Park

The Olympic Forest Park is a massive green space designed to balance the urban environment and provide a place for relaxation and recreation. Divided into northern and southern sections, it offers a serene escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.

  • Southern Section:
    Known for its lakes, bridges, and paved walking paths, this area is perfect for leisurely strolls and photography.
  • Northern Section:
    Featuring dense forests, hiking trails, and observation points, the northern area caters to nature enthusiasts.
  • Activities:
    Visitors can rent bicycles, jog along scenic routes, or enjoy picnics in designated areas.

The Olympic Torch Tower

The Olympic Torch Tower, also known as the Linglong Pagoda, is a prominent structure near the Bird’s Nest. It served as the base for the Olympic flame during the 2008 games.

  • Features:
    The tower offers an impressive view of the surrounding park, and its design reflects the harmony of traditional Chinese culture with modern aesthetics.

The Beijing National Indoor Stadium

This venue, nicknamed the "Fan," hosted gymnastics, handball, and trampoline events during the Olympics. It continues to serve as a site for sporting events and cultural performances.

  • Tours:
    Guided tours allow visitors to explore the stadium's facilities, including the seating areas and behind-the-scenes sections.

The Digital Beijing Building

Designed to resemble a stack of circuit boards, the Digital Beijing Building served as the data center during the 2008 Olympics. While it is not open to the public, its futuristic design makes it an interesting spot to admire and photograph.

Cultural and Recreational Opportunities

Olympic Cultural Corridor

This corridor showcases the history of the Olympic Games, with sculptures, murals, and installations celebrating the spirit of sportsmanship. It’s an excellent place to learn about the significance of the Olympics and China’s role in hosting the games.

Public Art Installations

Scattered throughout the park are numerous public art pieces, including sculptures and interactive installations. These artworks reflect themes of unity, competition, and environmental conservation.

Light and Fountain Shows

In the evenings, the park comes alive with light and fountain shows. The area near the Bird’s Nest and Water Cube often features synchronized water displays set to music, providing a delightful spectacle for visitors.

Practical Information for Visitors

Opening Hours

The park itself is open 24/7 for outdoor exploration, but individual attractions like the Bird’s Nest, Water Cube, and museums have specific opening hours. These generally range from 9:00 AM to 9:00 PM, though hours may vary seasonally.

Tickets and Admission

  • Bird’s Nest: Entrance tickets cost around 50 RMB ($7 USD), with additional fees for special exhibitions or activities.
  • Water Cube: Admission to the water park starts at 200 RMB ($30 USD).
  • Olympic Forest Park: Free entry.
  • Combined Tickets: Some agencies offer discounted packages for multiple attractions within the park.

Transportation

  • Subway: The Olympic Park can be easily accessed via Line 8 of the Beijing Subway. The Olympic Sports Center and Olympic Green stations are conveniently located near key attractions.
  • Buses: Several bus routes stop at the park, including lines 82, 510, and 839.
  • Walking and Biking: The park’s layout is pedestrian- and cyclist-friendly, making it easy to explore on foot or by renting a bike.

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring and Autumn: These seasons offer pleasant weather, ideal for exploring the park’s outdoor areas.
  • Evenings: Visiting after sunset allows you to enjoy the illuminated landmarks and fountain shows.

Tips for Visitors

  1. Wear Comfortable Shoes: The park is vast, so be prepared for a lot of walking.
  2. Stay Hydrated: Bring a water bottle, especially during summer. Vending machines and kiosks are available throughout the park.
  3. Plan Your Visit: Allocate at least half a day to explore the main attractions.
  4. Check Event Schedules: Some areas may host events or exhibitions, so check in advance to avoid missing out.
  5. Photography: Bring a good camera to capture the park’s architectural and natural beauty, particularly during the golden hours.

Nearby Attractions

While visiting Beijing Olympic Park, consider exploring nearby sites to make the most of your trip:

  • 798 Art District: A vibrant area showcasing contemporary art and galleries.
  • Beijing National Aquatics Center Ice and Snow World: A seasonal attraction offering ice skating and snow-related activities.

Beijing Olympic Park is a testament to the city’s ability to blend modern innovation with cultural heritage. Whether you’re a sports enthusiast, an architecture lover, or someone seeking a peaceful retreat, the park offers a diverse range of experiences that are sure to leave a lasting impression.
