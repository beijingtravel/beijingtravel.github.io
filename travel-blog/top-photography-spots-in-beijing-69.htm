Top Photography Spots in Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, the capital of China, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and modernity. Its blend of ancient architecture and contemporary landmarks makes it a paradise for photographers. Whether you're a professional with a DSLR or a traveler snapping shots with your smartphone, Beijing offers countless opportunities to capture breathtaking scenes. Here are some of the most popular photography spots in Beijing, along with tips to make the most of your visit.

The Forbidden City (Gugong)

The Forbidden City, or Gugong, is an iconic UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Beijingâ€™s most photogenic landmarks. This vast imperial palace is a masterpiece of traditional Chinese architecture and offers endless angles to explore.

  • Best Photography Spots:
    • The Meridian Gate (Wumen) for its grand entrance.
    • The Hall of Supreme Harmony (Taihe Dian) for intricate details and symmetry.
    • The Imperial Garden for a serene atmosphere.
  • Tips: Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and capture the golden sunlight on the red walls and tiled roofs.

The Great Wall of China (Badaling, Mutianyu, and Jinshanling)

No trip to Beijing is complete without photographing the Great Wall. Each section offers its own unique charm:

  • Badaling: Convenient and restored, ideal for classic shots.

  • Mutianyu: Known for fewer crowds and lush greenery.

  • Jinshanling: Perfect for more rugged and dramatic photography.

  • Best Time to Visit: Sunrise or sunset for soft lighting and fewer tourists.

  • Essential Shots: Capture the wall snaking through the hills, framed by seasonal landscapes like spring blossoms or autumn foliage.

Temple of Heaven (Tiantan)

This stunning temple complex is a symbol of harmony and balance. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (Qinian Dian), with its circular blue-tiled roof, is a must-photograph.

  • Photogenic Features: The symmetry of the temple, vibrant colors, and surrounding gardens.
  • Best Time: Morning, when local residents gather for tai chi, kite flying, or dancing, providing candid cultural moments.

Summer Palace (Yihe Yuan)

The Summer Palace is an enchanting mix of natural beauty and historical architecture. The sprawling grounds, dotted with pavilions, bridges, and Kunming Lake, offer endless photo opportunities.

  • Must-Capture Views:
    • The Long Corridor (Changlang) with its painted ceilings.
    • The Seventeen-Arch Bridge at sunset.
    • Reflections of Longevity Hill and the Tower of Buddhist Incense in the lake.
  • Pro Tip: Rent a boat for unique perspectives from the water.

Jingshan Park

For panoramic views of the Forbidden City and the surrounding Beijing skyline, Jingshan Park is unbeatable. Its central hill offers a vantage point that is particularly stunning at sunrise and sunset.

  • What to Photograph: The golden roofs of the Forbidden City from above, framed by the greenery of the park.
  • Best Season: Spring for blooming flowers or autumn for colorful foliage.

Hutongs

Beijingâ€™s hutongs are narrow alleyways brimming with charm and history. These neighborhoods provide a glimpse into traditional Beijing life and are perfect for street photography.

  • Recommended Hutongs:
    • Nanluoguxiang for trendy shops and cafes.
    • Wudaoying for quieter, artistic vibes.
  • Photo Ideas: Capture the intricate doorways, bicycles leaning against gray walls, or locals engaged in daily activities.

    • Wide shots that include the surrounding Olympic Park.

  • Tips: Use a tripod for night photography to capture long exposures.

Beihai Park

Beihai Park is one of Beijing's oldest and most beautiful parks, offering tranquil lake views and historical architecture.

  • Photographic Highlights:
    • The White Dagoba on Jade Flower Island.
    • Boats on Beihai Lake.
    • Lotus flowers in summer or snow-covered landscapes in winter.
  • Timing: Visit in the morning for soft lighting and peaceful scenes.

Wangfujing Street

Wangfujing is a bustling commercial area perfect for capturing the vibrancy of Beijing. Its food market and neon-lit shops provide dynamic urban photography opportunities.

  • Photo Ideas:
    • Street food vendors and their colorful displays.
    • Shoppers and the lively atmosphere.
  • Tips: Use a fast lens to capture low-light shots and motion blur.

798 Art District

For a mix of contemporary art and industrial charm, head to the 798 Art District. This area features galleries, graffiti, and unique installations that are perfect for creative photography.

  • What to Shoot:
    • Colorful murals and sculptures.
    • The contrast between industrial buildings and modern art.
  • Best Time: Late afternoon for vibrant colors and fewer crowds.

Tiananmen Square is a historic and iconic spot thatâ€™s perfect for photographing Beijingâ€™s grandeur.

Lama Temple (Yonghegong)

This Tibetan Buddhist temple is a haven for intricate details and spiritual ambiance. The richly decorated halls and incense-filled courtyards create a serene photographic experience.

  • Must-Capture Details:
    • Vibrant prayer flags.
    • Golden statues and carvings.
    • Devotees in prayer for authentic cultural shots.
  • Best Time: Morning, when the light filters through the incense smoke.

Fragrant Hills Park (Xiangshan)

If youâ€™re visiting in autumn, Fragrant Hills Park is a must. The parkâ€™s vibrant red maple leaves make it one of the most photogenic spots in Beijing.

  • Photo Ideas:

    • Panoramic views from the hilltop.
  • Timing: Late October to early November for peak foliage colors.

Zhongshan Park

Adjacent to the Forbidden City, Zhongshan Park is a peaceful escape with ancient cypress trees, flower beds, and classical architecture.

  • What to Photograph:
    • Seasonal blooms like tulips in spring.
    • The Temple of Longevity for architectural details.
  • Tips: Visit during festivals for colorful decorations and cultural performances.

Temple of Confucius and Guozijian Street

This less-crowded gem offers a tranquil setting with historical significance. The templeâ€™s ancient stone carvings and the neighboring Guozijian Street are a delight for photographers.

  • Must-See Features:
    • Rows of stone steles with Confucian teachings.
    • Traditional red lanterns along the street.
  • Pro Tip: Use a wide-angle lens to capture the expansive courtyards.
