Beijing Nightlife Costs: Bars, Clubs, and Entertainment

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing’s nightlife is a vibrant mix of traditional charm and modern energy. From hidden hutong bars to high-end nightclubs, the city offers something for every traveler. But how much does it really cost to enjoy Beijing after dark? Whether you're a budget backpacker or a luxury seeker, this guide breaks down the expenses you can expect at bars, clubs, and other entertainment spots.

The Bar Scene: From Cheap Beers to Craft Cocktails

1. Hutong Bars: Affordable and Authentic

Hidden in Beijing’s ancient alleyways, hutong bars are a must-visit for a local experience. These spots often have a cozy, laid-back vibe with prices that won’t break the bank.

  • Beer: A local draft (like Yanjing or Tsingtao) costs around ¥20–30 ($3–4.50).
  • Cocktails: Simple mixed drinks start at ¥40–60 ($6–9).
  • Cover Charge: Most hutong bars don’t have one, making them great for budget travelers.

Popular spots like Mao Mao Chong or Great Leap Brewing offer craft beers and creative cocktails in a relaxed setting.

2. Western-Style Bars: Mid-Range Prices

If you’re craving a taste of home, areas like Sanlitun and Gulou have plenty of expat-friendly bars.

  • Imported Beer: Expect to pay ¥40–60 ($6–9) for a bottle of Heineken or Corona.
  • Cocktails: Classics like mojitos or margaritas range from ¥60–100 ($9–15).
  • Happy Hours: Many bars offer discounts (e.g., 2-for-1 drinks) between 5–8 PM.

Places like The Local or Paddy O’Shea’s are great for socializing without overspending.

3. Rooftop & Luxury Bars: High-End Vibes

For stunning city views and premium drinks, rooftop bars like Migas or Atmosphere (at China World Summit Wing) are unbeatable—but come with a price tag.

  • Cocktails: ¥100–150 ($15–22) per drink.
  • Bottle Service: Champagne or premium spirits start at ¥800+ ($120+).
  • Dress Code: Some upscale bars enforce smart casual attire.

Clubbing in Beijing: Entry Fees, Bottles, and More

Beijing’s club scene is booming, with venues playing everything from EDM to hip-hop. Here’s what you’ll spend at different tiers of nightclubs.

1. Student-Friendly Clubs

Places like Propaganda or Sensation attract younger crowds with affordable drinks and no-frills entry.

  • Cover Charge: ¥50–100 ($7–15), often including a free drink.
  • Beer: ¥30–50 ($4.50–7.50).
  • Shots: ¥20–40 ($3–6).

These clubs are perfect if you just want to dance without splurging.

2. Mid-Tier Clubs: The Mainstream Scene

Clubs like Elements or Mix are where locals and expats mingle. Prices are higher but still reasonable.

  • Cover Charge: ¥100–200 ($15–30), sometimes waived for women or early arrivals.
  • Table/Bottle Service: A basic vodka or whiskey bottle starts at ¥800–1,500 ($120–220).
  • Cocktails: ¥60–100 ($9–15).

Weekends get crowded, so arrive early to avoid long lines.

3. VIP Clubs: The Luxury Experience

For those willing to spend big, clubs like One Third or OT (Omni) offer world-class DJs and lavish setups.

  • Table Minimums: ¥3,000–10,000+ ($450–1,500+) depending on the night.
  • Premium Bottles: Dom Pérignon or Johnnie Walker Blue Label can cost ¥5,000–20,000 ($750–3,000).
  • Guest Lists: Some promoters offer free entry if you reserve in advance.

Other Nighttime Entertainment

1. Live Music Venues

Beijing has a thriving indie music scene. Check out School Bar or Yugong Yishan for local bands.

  • Ticket Prices: ¥50–150 ($7–22) for most shows.
  • Drinks: Beers around ¥30–50 ($4.50–7.50).

2. KTV (Karaoke)

A favorite pastime in China, KTV ranges from budget to ultra-luxe.

  • Hourly Rates: ¥50–200 ($7–30) per room.
  • All-Night Packages: ¥300–800 ($45–120) including drinks.

3. Late-Night Eats

After-party snacks are a must. Street food like jianbing (savory crepes) costs ¥10–15 ($1.50–2.50), while 24-hour diners like Lush serve Western comfort food for ¥50–100 ($7–15).

Money-Saving Tips for Beijing Nightlife

  • Happy Hours: Many bars offer discounts before 8 PM.
  • Guest Lists: Follow club promoters on WeChat for free entry deals.
  • Pre-Gaming: Buy drinks from convenience stores (like 7-Eleven) before heading out.
  • Weekdays vs. Weekends: Clubs are cheaper and less crowded Monday–Thursday.

Whether you’re sipping ¥10 beers in a hutong or popping bottles in a VIP booth, Beijing’s nightlife has options for every budget. Plan ahead, watch for deals, and dive into the city’s after-dark energy!
