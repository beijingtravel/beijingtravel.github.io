Summer Escape: A Refreshing 4-Day Beijing Itinerary

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, China’s bustling capital, is a city where ancient history and modern vibrancy collide. Summer might bring the heat, but it also offers long daylight hours perfect for exploring. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an adventurer, this 4-day itinerary ensures you experience the best of Beijing while staying refreshed.

Day 1: Imperial Grandeur & Hutong Charm

Morning: The Forbidden City & Jingshan Park

Start your journey at the Forbidden City, the world’s largest imperial palace complex. Arrive early to beat the crowds and the midday sun. Wander through its grand halls and courtyards, imagining life during the Ming and Qing dynasties.

Afterward, head to Jingshan Park just north of the palace. Climb to the top for a panoramic view of the Forbidden City’s golden rooftops—a perfect photo op.

Afternoon: Lunch in a Courtyard & Hutong Exploration

Escape the heat with lunch at TRB Hutong, a refined restaurant set in a restored temple. Then, dive into Beijing’s historic hutongs (narrow alleyways). Rent a bike or take a rickshaw tour to see traditional siheyuan (courtyard homes). Stop by Nanluoguxiang, a lively hutong filled with quirky shops and cafés.

Evening: Summer Night at Houhai

As the sun sets, head to Houhai Lake, a hotspot for nightlife. Stroll along the water, enjoy live music at a lakeside bar, or try chuanr (grilled skewers) from a street vendor.

Day 2: The Great Wall & Summer Retreat

Morning: Mutianyu Great Wall

Escape the city early for Mutianyu, a less crowded but equally stunning section of the Great Wall. Hike the restored towers or take the cable car for breathtaking views. Pro tip: Bring a handheld fan and plenty of water—summer hikes can be sweaty!

Afternoon: Lunch with a View & Summer Palace

Refuel at The Schoolhouse, a farm-to-table restaurant near Mutianyu. Then, head back to the city for the Summer Palace, a Qing-era retreat with lush gardens and Kunming Lake. Rent a paddleboat or simply relax in the shade of willow trees.

Evening: Peking Duck Feast

No Beijing trip is complete without Peking duck. Book a table at Da Dong or Quanjude for crispy skin and succulent meat wrapped in thin pancakes.

Day 3: Art, Culture & Local Flavors

Morning: 798 Art District

Beat the heat indoors at 798 Art Zone, a repurposed factory complex turned contemporary art hub. Galleries like UCCA showcase cutting-edge exhibitions. Don’t miss the quirky street art and cafés.

Afternoon: Temple of Heaven & Local Market

Visit the Temple of Heaven, where emperors once prayed for good harvests. The park is a favorite among locals for tai chi and card games. Nearby, explore Tiantan Donglu Market for souvenirs like silk scarves or tea.

Evening: Acrobatics Show & Night Market

Cap the day with a thrilling acrobatics show at Chaoyang Theatre. Then, head to Wangfujing Snack Street for bizarre yet fun street food—think scorpion skewers or candied hawthorns.

Day 4: Modern Beijing & Relaxation

Morning: Olympic Park & Water Cube

Visit the Bird’s Nest and Water Cube from the 2008 Olympics. The Water Cube now houses a fun water park—perfect for cooling off!

Afternoon: Sanlitun Shopping & Cafés

Indulge in retail therapy at Sanlitun, Beijing’s trendy district. Boutiques like Chao and Joy City offer fashion and tech. Recharge at % Arabica or Berry Beans with an iced latte.

Evening: Rooftop Drinks & Farewell

End your trip with cocktails at Migas Mercado, a rooftop bar with skyline views. Toast to an unforgettable summer escape!

This itinerary balances adventure, culture, and relaxation—ensuring you leave Beijing refreshed and inspired. Pack light, stay hydrated, and embrace the city’s dynamic spirit!
