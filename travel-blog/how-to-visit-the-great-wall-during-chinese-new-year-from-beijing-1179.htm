How to Visit the Great Wall During Chinese New Year from Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

The Great Wall of China is a must-see destination for any traveler, but visiting during Chinese New Year (Chunjie) adds a unique cultural layer to the experience. While the holiday brings crowds and logistical challenges, it also offers festive vibes, fewer international tourists, and a chance to witness local traditions. Here’s your ultimate guide to making the most of your Great Wall adventure during this special time.

Why Visit the Great Wall During Chinese New Year?

Chinese New Year is the most important holiday in China, marked by family reunions, fireworks, and vibrant celebrations. While many locals travel home, the Great Wall remains open—and with fewer international visitors, you might even snag quieter moments on certain sections. Plus, the winter scenery (think snow-dusted ramparts) is breathtaking.

Pros:

  • Unique Atmosphere: Red lanterns, seasonal decorations, and occasional lion dances near tourist hubs.
  • Fewer Crowds: Compared to peak seasons like Golden Week, some sections are less packed.
  • Winter Beauty: Snow transforms the Wall into a picturesque wonderland (weather permitting).

Cons:

  • Limited Services: Some restaurants, shops, or transport options may close early or operate on reduced schedules.
  • Cold Weather: Temperatures can drop below freezing, so pack accordingly.

Best Sections to Visit During Chunjie

Not all parts of the Great Wall are equal during the holiday. Here are the top picks:

1. Mutianyu

Why Go?
- Well-restored with fewer crowds than Badaling.
- Cable car and toboggan ride (fun for families).
- Stunning winter views.

Logistics:
- Open year-round; arrive early to avoid midday tours.
- Book tickets online in advance (some platforms offer discounts).

2. Jinshanling

Why Go?
- Semi-wild section with rugged beauty.
- Ideal for photographers seeking snow-covered landscapes.

Logistics:
- Less commercialized; pack snacks/water.
- Hire a driver from Beijing (public transport is sparse during Chunjie).

3. Badaling

Why Go?
- Most accessible (direct buses from Beijing).
- Festive decorations and activities for the holiday.

Logistics:
- Expect larger domestic tourist groups.
- Visit on weekdays to dodge the biggest crowds.

Getting There from Beijing

Transport options vary during the holiday. Here’s what to expect:

By Public Transport

  • Bus 877 to Badaling: Runs daily but may have reduced frequency.
  • Tourist Bus Lines: Check for holiday schedules (e.g., Mutianyu Express).

By Private Car/Driver

  • Pros: Flexibility, comfort, and door-to-door service.
  • Cons: Higher cost; book drivers in advance (many take time off).

Group Tours

Many operators run Chunjie-specific tours, often including:
- Round-trip transport.
- Guided hikes with cultural insights.
- Optional add-ons like hot pot lunches.

What to Pack for a Winter Visit

The Great Wall in winter demands preparation:
- Layered Clothing: Thermal base layers, insulated jacket, gloves, and a hat.
- Sturdy Footwear: Ice-resistant boots (some paths get slippery).
- Snacks/Water: Limited vendors may be open.
- Camera: Capture the frosty panoramas.

Cultural Etiquette During Chunjie

Respect local customs to enhance your experience:
- Red Envelopes (Hongbao): If offered (rare for tourists), accept with both hands.
- Fireworks: Common at night; don’t be startled!
- Greetings: Learn basic phrases like "Xīnnián kuàilè" (Happy New Year).

Insider Tips

  • Avoid Day 1-3 of Chunjie: Many locals prioritize family time; attractions are quieter but services are limited.
  • Check Weather Alerts: Snow can temporarily close sections.
  • Combine with Other Activities: Pair your trip with a Beijing hutong tour or temple fair visit.

Where to Stay Nearby

Extend your trip with these options:
- Gubei Water Town: A charming resort near Simatai with lantern-lit streets.
- Beijing Downtown: Hotels like The Peninsula offer luxury and easy transport links.

Final Notes

Visiting the Great Wall during Chinese New Year is a trade-off between cultural immersion and logistical hurdles. Plan ahead, embrace the festive spirit, and you’ll leave with unforgettable memories—and maybe a few lucky red envelopes!
