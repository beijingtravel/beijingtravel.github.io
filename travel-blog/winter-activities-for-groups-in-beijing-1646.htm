Winter Activities for Groups in Beijing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing transforms into a winter wonderland when the temperatures drop, offering a wide range of exciting activities perfect for groups. Whether you're traveling with friends, family, or colleagues, the city provides unforgettable experiences—from ice skating on historic lakes to exploring snow-covered temples. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best winter activities for groups in Beijing.

Outdoor Adventures

Ice Skating at Houhai Lake

Houhai Lake, located in the heart of Beijing’s historic hutongs, turns into a massive ice rink during winter. Groups can rent skates and glide across the frozen surface while enjoying views of traditional courtyard houses and lively bars nearby. The atmosphere is especially magical at night when the area is lit up with lanterns.

Skiing at Nanshan Ski Village

For those craving adrenaline, Nanshan Ski Village is a top destination. Just an hour’s drive from downtown Beijing, this resort offers slopes for all skill levels. Groups can take lessons together, race down the hills, or simply enjoy the snowy scenery. The après-ski scene includes cozy lodges with hot drinks and snacks.

Snow Hiking on the Great Wall

Few experiences compare to hiking the Great Wall in winter. Sections like Mutianyu and Jinshanling are less crowded, offering breathtaking views of snow-covered mountains. Groups can organize guided hikes, capture stunning photos, and warm up with thermoses of hot tea along the way.

Cultural Experiences

Temple Fairs During Spring Festival

Beijing’s temple fairs (miaohui) are a must-visit during Lunar New Year. Locations like Ditan Park and Longtan Park host vibrant events featuring traditional performances, handicrafts, and street food. Groups can immerse themselves in the festive spirit while trying local delicacies like tanghulu (candied fruit) and roasted chestnuts.

Hot Pot Feasts

Winter in Beijing calls for hot pot, and group dining is the best way to enjoy it. Popular chains like Haidilao offer interactive dining experiences with personalized broths and entertaining noodle-pulling performances. Spicy Sichuan-style or mild mushroom broth—there’s something for everyone.

Peking Opera with a Modern Twist

For a cultural night out, groups can attend a Peking Opera performance at Liyuan Theatre. The elaborate costumes and dramatic storytelling are mesmerizing, and some venues even offer backstage tours or workshops where visitors can try on traditional makeup.

Indoor Entertainment

Escape Rooms with Chinese Themes

Escape rooms have become a popular group activity in Beijing, with many featuring themes based on Chinese history or mythology. Teams can solve puzzles in settings like an ancient imperial palace or a legendary martial arts dojo. It’s a fun way to bond while engaging with local culture.

Karaoke (KTV) Nights

No group trip to Beijing is complete without a KTV session. Private rooms equipped with high-tech sound systems allow everyone to sing their hearts out in Mandarin, English, or any language they choose. Many KTV spots also offer all-you-can-eat snacks and drinks.

DIY Dumpling Workshops

Learning to make dumplings (jiaozi) is a hands-on winter activity perfect for groups. Many cooking schools and restaurants offer workshops where participants can mix fillings, fold wrappers, and enjoy their creations afterward. It’s a delicious way to experience Chinese culinary traditions.

Seasonal Events

Ice and Snow Festivals

The Beijing Ice and Snow Festival, held in venues like Shichahai or Olympic Park, features massive ice sculptures, ice slides, and even ice mazes. Groups can wander through dazzling displays and participate in games like ice curling or sledding.

Lantern Displays at Yu Garden

Though originally a Shanghai tradition, Beijing’s Yu Garden (or similar venues) often hosts stunning lantern displays during winter. Intricate light installations depict mythical creatures and historical scenes, creating a magical atmosphere for evening strolls.

Winter Hot Springs

After a day of outdoor activities, groups can unwind at one of Beijing’s nearby hot springs. Resorts like Nine Clouds Hot Spring offer private pools, spa treatments, and scenic mountain views—ideal for relaxation and conversation.

Practical Tips for Group Travel

  • Dress in layers—Beijing’s winter can be harsh, so thermal wear and waterproof boots are essential.
  • Book in advance—Popular activities like skiing and temple fairs can get crowded, especially during holidays.
  • Use group discounts—Many attractions offer special rates for larger parties.
  • Stay connected—Apps like WeChat or Didi make coordinating transportation and payments easier.

From thrilling snow sports to cozy cultural experiences, Beijing in winter has something for every group. The mix of adventure, tradition, and modern entertainment ensures unforgettable memories.
