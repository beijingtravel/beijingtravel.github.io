Beijing Weekend Getaway: Top 10 Must-Visit Spots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Escaping the hustle and bustle of Beijing for a weekend doesn’t mean you have to travel far. The capital’s surroundings are packed with breathtaking landscapes, cultural gems, and hidden retreats perfect for a quick recharge. Whether you’re into hiking, history, or just unwinding in nature, here are the top 10 must-visit spots for your next Beijing weekend getaway.

1. The Great Wall at Mutianyu

Why Visit?

Mutianyu offers one of the most scenic and less crowded sections of the Great Wall. Unlike Badaling, it retains a more authentic feel with lush greenery and stunning views.

What to Do

  • Hike along the restored and wild sections.
  • Take the cable car or toboggan for a thrilling descent.
  • Capture Instagram-worthy shots from Watchtower 14.

Pro Tip

Visit during sunrise or sunset for magical lighting and fewer crowds.

2. Fragrant Hills Park (Xiangshan)

Why Visit?

A favorite autumn destination, Fragrant Hills dazzles with fiery red maple leaves. But it’s also a year-round escape with serene temples and hiking trails.

What to Do

  • Climb to Incense Burner Peak for panoramic views.
  • Explore the tranquil Azure Clouds Temple.
  • Visit in October-November for peak foliage.

Pro Tip

Weekdays are quieter—perfect for a peaceful stroll.

3. Gubei Water Town

Why Visit?

This charming canal town mimics the aesthetic of southern China with cobblestone streets, lantern-lit alleys, and hot springs.

What to Do

  • Wander the ancient-style streets and bridges.
  • Soak in a rooftop hot spring with Great Wall views.
  • Catch the nightly light and water show.

Pro Tip

Pair your visit with a trip to nearby Simatai Great Wall for a full experience.

4. Shidu Scenic Area

Why Visit?

Known as the "Guilin of the North," Shidu boasts karst landscapes, winding rivers, and adrenaline-pumping activities.

What to Do

  • Try bamboo rafting on the Juma River.
  • Bungee jump or zip-line for thrill-seekers.
  • Explore the eerie underground caves.

Pro Tip

Spring and autumn offer the best weather for outdoor adventures.

5. Tanzhe Temple

Why Visit?

One of Beijing’s oldest Buddhist temples, Tanzhe is surrounded by ancient cypress trees and exudes tranquility.

What to Do

  • Admire the grand Mahavira Hall.
  • Walk the peaceful forested paths behind the temple.
  • Savor a vegetarian meal at the temple’s restaurant.

Pro Tip

Arrive early to enjoy the misty morning atmosphere.

6. Yanqi Lake

Why Visit?

This man-made lake near Mutianyu is perfect for a laid-back retreat with luxury resorts and water activities.

What to Do

  • Kayak or paddleboard on the crystal-clear water.
  • Stay at the Kempinski Hotel for lakeside luxury.
  • Cycle around the scenic lakeside trails.

Pro Tip

Visit in spring when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

7. Cuandixia Village

Why Visit?

A beautifully preserved Ming-era village, Cuandixia feels like stepping back in time with its stone houses and mountain backdrop.

What to Do

  • Explore the narrow alleys and courtyard homes.
  • Hike to the hilltop for a village panorama.
  • Try local specialties like wild vegetable pancakes.

Pro Tip

Stay overnight in a traditional guesthouse for an immersive experience.

8. Baihuashan Natural Reserve

Why Visit?

A paradise for hikers, Baihuashan offers rugged trails, wildflowers, and sweeping vistas.

What to Do

  • Trek to the summit for breathtaking views.
  • Camp overnight under the stars.
  • Spot rare wildlife like pheasants and foxes.

Pro Tip

Bring sturdy shoes—the trails can be steep and rocky.

9. Longqing Gorge

Why Visit?

Dubbed the "Three Gorges of Beijing," this dramatic canyon is ideal for boating and ice lantern festivals in winter.

What to Do

  • Take a boat ride through the towering cliffs.
  • Visit during January-February for the Ice Festival.
  • Walk across the thrilling suspension bridge.

Pro Tip

Winter visits require warm clothing but reward with magical ice sculptures.

10. Miyun Reservoir

Why Visit?

Beijing’s largest reservoir is a serene spot for picnics, cycling, and birdwatching.

What to Do

  • Rent a bike and ride the scenic loop.
  • Enjoy fresh fish at lakeside restaurants.
  • Visit nearby Nanshan Ski Resort in winter.

Pro Tip

Sunset views over the water are unforgettable.

Each of these destinations offers a unique way to unwind and explore beyond Beijing’s city limits. Whether you’re craving adventure, culture, or relaxation, there’s a perfect weekend escape waiting for you.
