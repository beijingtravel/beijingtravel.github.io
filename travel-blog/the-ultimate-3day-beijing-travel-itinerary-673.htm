The Ultimate 3-Day Beijing Travel Itinerary

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, the capital of China, is a mesmerizing blend of ancient history and modern dynamism. With its imperial palaces, bustling hutongs, and cutting-edge architecture, the city offers an unforgettable travel experience. If you’ve only got three days to explore, this itinerary will help you hit the must-see spots while diving into local culture, food, and hidden gems.

Day 1: Imperial Grandeur & Hutong Charm

Morning: The Forbidden City & Tiananmen Square

Start your Beijing adventure at Tiananmen Square, the world’s largest public square. Snap a photo in front of the Mao Zedong Portrait before heading into the Forbidden City (故宫, Gùgōng). This UNESCO-listed imperial palace complex, home to 24 emperors, is a masterpiece of Ming and Qing dynasty architecture. Don’t miss the Hall of Supreme Harmony and the Imperial Garden.

Pro Tip: Book tickets online in advance to skip the long queues.

Afternoon: Jingshan Park & Lunch in a Hutong

After exiting the Forbidden City, climb Jingshan Park for a panoramic view of the palace’s golden rooftops. Then, wander into the nearby Nanluoguxiang Hutong, a lively alleyway filled with boutique shops and traditional courtyard homes. Stop for lunch at Mr. Shi’s Dumplings—their jiaozi (dumplings) are legendary.

Evening: Drum & Bell Towers + Houhai Nightlife

As the sun sets, visit the Drum and Bell Towers, where ancient timekeeping instruments once marked the hours. Then, stroll to Houhai Lake, a hotspot for bars, live music, and lakeside dining. Try Peking duck at Liqun Roast Duck Restaurant, a no-frills local favorite.

Day 2: The Great Wall & Art Districts

Morning: Mutianyu Great Wall

Rise early for a day trip to Mutianyu Great Wall, less crowded than Badaling but equally breathtaking. Hike or take the cable car up, then walk along the restored battlements surrounded by lush forests. The watchtowers offer perfect photo ops.

Pro Tip: Wear comfortable shoes—some sections are steep!

Afternoon: 798 Art Zone

Return to the city and head to 798 Art Zone, a former factory turned avant-garde arts district. Galleries like UCCA Center for Contemporary Art showcase cutting-edge Chinese artists. Grab coffee at At Café and browse quirky souvenir shops.

Evening: Sanlitun & Craft Cocktails

For dinner, explore Sanlitun, Beijing’s trendy expat hub. Try fusion dishes at The Georg or spicy Sichuan at Chuan Ban. Cap the night with cocktails at Janes + Hooch, a speakeasy-style bar with a retro vibe.

Day 3: Temples, Parks & Local Markets

Morning: Temple of Heaven & Local Breakfast

Begin at the Temple of Heaven, where emperors once prayed for good harvests. Watch locals practicing tai chi or playing jianzi (Chinese hacky sack) in the surrounding park. Then, grab a jianbing (savory crepe) from a street vendor—it’s the ultimate Beijing breakfast.

Afternoon: Summer Palace & Kunming Lake

Next, escape to the Summer Palace, a sprawling imperial garden with pavilions, bridges, and Kunming Lake. Rent a paddleboat or walk along the Long Corridor, adorned with intricate paintings.

Evening: Wangfujing Snack Street & Peking Opera

Wrap up your trip with a visit to Wangfujing Snack Street, where adventurous eaters can try scorpion skewers or stinky tofu. For culture lovers, catch a Peking Opera show at Li Yuan Theater, featuring vibrant costumes and traditional music.

This whirlwind three-day itinerary packs in Beijing’s highlights while leaving room for serendipitous discoveries. Whether you’re marveling at ancient wonders or sipping cocktails in a hutong, Beijing’s magic is unforgettable.
