Avoiding Fake Taxis in Beijing: Safe Transportation Tips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage and countless attractions, from the Great Wall to the Forbidden City. However, like any major city, it has its share of scams—especially when it comes to transportation. One of the most common issues travelers face is encountering fake taxis. These unlicensed vehicles can overcharge, take longer routes, or even put passengers at risk. Here’s how to spot and avoid fake taxis while ensuring a safe and smooth travel experience in Beijing.

How to Identify Fake Taxis in Beijing

Fake taxis often look convincing at first glance, but there are telltale signs to watch for:

1. Check the License Plate and Taxi Branding

Legitimate taxis in Beijing have license plates starting with "京B" (Jīng B). Avoid vehicles with other prefixes or no branding at all. Licensed taxis also display company logos, such as Shouqi (首汽) or Jinjian (金建).

2. Look for a Meter and Driver ID

Real taxis must have a working meter and a visible driver identification card with a photo. If the driver refuses to use the meter or the ID is missing, it’s a red flag.

3. Be Wary of Aggressive Solicitation

Fake taxi drivers often lurk near tourist hotspots like Tiananmen Square or Wangfujing Street, aggressively offering rides. Licensed taxis usually queue at designated stands rather than approaching pedestrians.

Safe Alternatives to Hailing Taxis on the Street

Instead of risking an unlicensed ride, consider these safer options:

1. Use Ride-Hailing Apps

Apps like Didi Chuxing (China’s Uber equivalent) are reliable and track your route. Payments are cashless, reducing scams.

2. Book Through Your Hotel

Hotels often partner with trusted taxi services. Ask the concierge to arrange a ride.

3. Take the Subway or Public Buses

Beijing’s subway system is efficient, cheap, and scam-free. Major attractions are well-connected.

What to Do If You’re Already in a Fake Taxi

If you realize too late that you’re in an unlicensed cab:

  • Stay calm and avoid confrontation.
  • Take note of the license plate (if visible) and report it to local authorities.
  • Use a map app to track your route and ensure you’re not being taken the long way.

Additional Safety Tips for Transportation in Beijing

  • Carry small bills—some drivers claim they can’t make change.
  • Avoid unmarked cars at airports—only use official taxi queues.
  • Learn basic Mandarin phrases like "Dǎbiǎo ma?" (打表吗? – Do you use the meter?) to assert control.

By staying vigilant and using trusted transportation methods, you can explore Beijing without falling victim to fake taxis. Safe travels!
