Summer Palace Beijing: Ticket Prices, Hours, and How to Get There

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

The Summer Palace in Beijing is not merely a destination; it is an epic poem written in pavilions, pagodas, and the still waters of Kunming Lake. It stands as a majestic testament to Chinese imperial garden design, a place where nature and human artistry have danced in harmony for centuries. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, it offers a breathtaking escape from the city's relentless pace. If you are planning a trip to Beijing, a visit to the Summer Palace is non-negotiable. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the essential details—ticket prices, opening hours, and how to get there—while immersing you in the magic that makes this place a perpetual travel hotspot.

A Stroll Through Time: The Imperial Retreat

Before delving into the practicalities, it's essential to understand the soul of this place. The Summer Palace, or Yihe Yuan, translates to the "Garden of Nurtured Harmony." Its primary purpose was to serve as a luxurious escape for the imperial family from the sweltering summer heat of the Forbidden City. The site's history is deeply intertwined with the formidable Empress Dowager Cixi, who famously diverted funds intended for the Chinese navy to reconstruct and expand the palace in the late 19th century.

The park is a masterpiece of landscape gardening, covering over 700 acres. The design philosophy follows the traditional Chinese principle of creating a "microcosm of the universe," incorporating elements of water, hills, and architecture to represent a perfect balance. The centerpieces are the man-made Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill, around which all other attractions are elegantly arranged.

Planning Your Visit: Tickets and Hours

Proper planning is the key to unlocking a seamless and enjoyable experience at the Summer Palace. The complex is vast, and understanding the ticketing structure will help you make the most of your day.

Summer Palace Ticket Prices

The Summer Palace uses a tiered ticketing system, which can be a bit confusing at first glance. Essentially, you can purchase a basic entry ticket that grants access to the main park areas, or you can opt for a through ticket that includes entry to all the major interior attractions within the park.

  • Low Season (November 1st - March 31st)

    • Park Entry Only: 20 RMB
    • Through Ticket (Including Dehe Garden, Tower of Buddhist Incense, Wenchang Hall, Suzhou Street): 50 RMB

  • High Season (April 1st - October 31st)

    • Park Entry Only: 30 RMB
    • Through Ticket (Including Dehe Garden, Tower of Buddhist Incense, Wenchang Hall, Suzhou Street): 60 RMB

Important Notes: * The through ticket offers excellent value if you plan to see the main historical buildings. If you prefer a more relaxed stroll through the gardens and along the lake, the basic park entry is sufficient. * Some smaller exhibits or halls inside the park may require an additional, separate fee (usually 5-10 RMB). * Tickets can be purchased at the entrance gates. However, to avoid long queues, especially during Chinese national holidays and weekends, it is highly recommended to book online in advance through the official Summer Palace WeChat channel or reputable travel platforms like Trip.com.

Summer Palace Opening Hours

The opening hours also vary slightly between seasons. The park itself opens early, a fantastic time for photographers and those seeking tranquility.

  • Park Opening Hours:

    • High Season (April 1st - October 31st): 6:30 AM - 6:00 PM
    • Low Season (November 1st - March 31st): 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM

  • Attraction Opening Hours (for buildings like the Tower of Buddhist Incense):

    • High Season: 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM
    • Low Season: 9:00 AM - 4:00 PM

The park often remains open later in the evening during the summer, allowing for beautiful sunset views over Kunming Lake. Always double-check the official website for the most current information before your visit.

Navigating Your Way to the Summer Palace

Beijing's extensive and efficient public transportation system makes getting to the Summer Palace remarkably straightforward. While taxis are available, the subway is often the fastest and most reliable option, avoiding the city's infamous traffic jams.

By Subway (The Recommended Way)

The Beijing Subway is your best friend. Take Line 4 (the brown line) and get off at Xiyuan Station (Exit C2) or Beigongmen Station (Exit D). * From Xiyuan Station: A short 10-minute walk will bring you to the East Palace Gate, the main and most ornate entrance. * From Beigongmen Station: This exit places you right at the North Palace Gate. This is a great starting point if you wish to explore Suzhou Street first.

By Bus

Several public bus routes serve the Summer Palace. Key bus stops include "Summer Palace East Gate" and "Summer Palace West Gate." Some useful routes are 303, 331, 332, 346, 384, 394, 563, 584, and 601. Using a navigation app like Baidu Maps or Amap is crucial for finding the correct bus from your location.

By Taxi or Ride-Hailing

You can simply tell the driver "Yihe Yuan" (Summer Palace). The most common drop-off point is the East Palace Gate. While convenient, this option can be more expensive and is subject to traffic conditions. Ride-hailing apps like Didi are widely used and convenient.

Unmissable Highlights and Travel Hotspots Within the Park

Once inside, the sheer scale can be overwhelming. Here are the must-see spots that consistently capture the attention of visitors and photographers alike.

The Long Corridor (Chang Lang)

Stretching for 728 meters along the northern shore of Kunming Lake, the Long Corridor is a marvel in itself. It is the longest covered walkway in the world and is famous for its beautifully painted beams. Each crossbeam features a different scene from Chinese history, mythology, and literature, making it a "walking gallery" of traditional art.

Tower of Buddhist Incense (Foxiang Ge)

This magnificent octagonal tower perched high on the slope of Longevity Hill is the iconic symbol of the Summer Palace. The climb is steep, but the panoramic view from the top, overlooking the entire complex and Kunming Lake, is absolutely worth the effort. It is the central building in the imperial garden and a testament to its spiritual significance.

Kunming Lake and the Marble Boat

The vast expanse of Kunming Lake is the heart of the garden. You can enjoy a leisurely boat ride on the lake, which offers a unique perspective of the palace grounds. At the northwestern shore sits the famous Marble Boat (Qingyan Fang), a stone structure built by Empress Dowager Cixi. It is a fascinating piece of architecture that has become a symbol of the decay of the Qing dynasty.

Suzhou Street

Located at the northern foot of Longevity Hill, this is a charming recreation of a traditional waterside market street from Suzhou. The shops are built along a narrow canal, and in imperial times, eunuchs and palace maids would role-play as shopkeepers and customers for the entertainment of the emperor and his court. Today, you can stroll through it and imagine the scene.

Pro-Tips for an Enhanced Experience

  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: You will be doing a lot of walking, often on uneven surfaces. This is not a place for high heels or new, unworn shoes.
  • Allocate Enough Time: To truly appreciate the Summer Palace, dedicate at least 4-5 hours. A full day is ideal for a relaxed exploration.
  • Bring Water and Snacks: While there are vendors and a couple of restaurants inside, they can be pricey. Carrying your own water is a must.
  • Go Early or Late: To avoid the largest crowds, aim to arrive right at opening time or later in the afternoon. The light is also best for photography during these times.
  • Consider a Guided Tour: The history and symbolism are rich. A knowledgeable guide can bring the stories of Empress Dowager Cixi and the Qing court to life, adding immense depth to your visit.
  • Combine with Other Attractions: The Summer Palace is located in the northwestern part of Beijing. It can be conveniently combined with a visit to the Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan), which is just a short distance away, offering a poignant contrast between restored glory and majestic ruins.
