Unique Handicrafts in Beijing Worth Purchasing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city rich in history and culture. With its ancient traditions and modern development, the city offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore a diverse range of handicrafts that represent China’s cultural heritage. Whether you are a collector, an art enthusiast, or simply someone looking for a memorable souvenir, Beijing has something special to offer. From intricate woodwork to delicate silk embroidery, here are some of the most unique handicrafts in Beijing that are definitely worth purchasing.

1. Peking Opera Masks

One of the most iconic symbols of Chinese culture is the Peking Opera mask. Peking Opera is a traditional form of Chinese theater that combines singing, acting, and acrobatics. The vibrant masks used in performances are highly decorative and represent various characters, from heroes to villains. These masks are painted in bright colors and feature elaborate designs that are meant to convey the character's personality and role in the opera.

When visiting Beijing, you can find beautifully crafted Peking Opera masks in markets and shops throughout the city. These masks are often made of wood or paper-mâché and are painted by hand, making each one unique. Purchasing a Peking Opera mask is a fantastic way to bring a piece of China’s theatrical tradition home with you.

2. Jingdezhen Porcelain

Jingdezhen, a city located in the Jiangxi province of China, is famous for its porcelain production, and its pieces are considered some of the finest in the world. Though Jingdezhen is not located in Beijing, the capital city is home to many stores that sell Jingdezhen porcelain products. This porcelain is known for its delicate texture, beautiful glaze, and intricate designs. It is often decorated with traditional Chinese motifs such as peacocks, dragons, and flowers.

You can find a variety of Jingdezhen porcelain items in Beijing, including tea sets, vases, and figurines. These pieces are often hand-painted and feature fine details that make them stand out as exquisite works of art. Whether you are looking for a traditional tea set or a decorative vase, Jingdezhen porcelain is a timeless souvenir that reflects China’s long history of porcelain craftsmanship.

3. Beijing Cloisonné

Cloisonné is a traditional enamelware technique that originated in China over 2,000 years ago. The technique involves applying colored enamel to metal using thin wires to separate different sections, creating intricate and vibrant patterns. Beijing cloisonné is particularly famous for its exceptional quality and detailed designs, often featuring traditional Chinese motifs such as dragons, flowers, and clouds.

Beijing cloisonné items are often seen in the form of vases, jewelry, bowls, and decorative plates. These colorful and elegant pieces are a popular choice for tourists looking for a unique and high-quality handicraft. The city’s ancient history in cloisonné production means you can find some truly remarkable pieces that showcase the beauty of traditional Chinese art.

4. Chinese Silk Embroidery

Silk embroidery is another unique handicraft that is highly regarded in Beijing. China has a long history of silk production, and the art of silk embroidery dates back over 1,000 years. Beijing is home to many shops and galleries where visitors can purchase finely embroidered silk items, including tapestries, tablecloths, clothing, and even framed artwork.

The designs on these silk pieces often feature traditional Chinese motifs, such as peonies, birds, and landscapes. Some of the most intricate silk embroidery is hand-stitched, making it a true work of art. Many of these pieces are created by skilled artisans who have honed their craft over generations. If you are looking for something elegant and truly representative of Chinese culture, Chinese silk embroidery is a wonderful option to consider.

5. Chinese Calligraphy Brushes and Paper

For those interested in traditional Chinese arts, purchasing a set of Chinese calligraphy brushes and rice paper is a great way to take home a piece of Chinese culture. Calligraphy is highly revered in China, and Beijing is home to many shops that sell high-quality calligraphy supplies. A calligraphy brush is an essential tool for writing Chinese characters, and it is often made from a mix of animal hairs, such as wolf, goat, and rabbit.

In addition to the brushes, you can also purchase rice paper, which is a special type of paper used for Chinese calligraphy and painting. The paper has a smooth texture that allows ink to flow easily, creating beautiful brushstrokes. If you are interested in learning calligraphy or simply want to bring home a beautiful set of writing tools, a Chinese calligraphy brush and rice paper set is an ideal choice.

6. Carved Lacquerware

Lacquerware is a traditional Chinese handicraft that involves applying layers of lacquer to wood, metal, or bamboo and then carving intricate designs into the surface. Beijing is known for its exquisite carved lacquerware, which can be found in the form of jewelry boxes, trays, and decorative plates. These pieces are often carved with traditional designs, such as dragons, flowers, and birds, and are highly prized for their beauty and craftsmanship.

Carved lacquerware is made by applying multiple layers of lacquer to the surface, then carving into the lacquer to reveal the design. The final product is highly polished and has a glossy finish, making it a visually striking piece. If you’re looking for a unique and elegant gift or souvenir, Beijing’s carved lacquerware is a perfect choice.

7. Hand-Painted Fans

Fans have been used in Chinese culture for centuries, both as a functional tool and as a form of art. In Beijing, you can find hand-painted fans that feature beautiful scenes from traditional Chinese culture, such as landscapes, flowers, and animals. These fans are often made from silk or paper and are mounted on bamboo or wooden frames. The designs are painted by hand, making each fan a unique work of art.

Chinese fans are not only decorative but also serve as symbols of elegance and grace. Many visitors to Beijing purchase hand-painted fans as souvenirs or gifts because they are lightweight and easy to pack. Whether you choose a fan with intricate floral designs or one featuring scenes from classical Chinese mythology, hand-painted fans are a great way to bring home a beautiful piece of Chinese art.

8. Beijing Paper-Cut Art

Paper-cutting is a traditional Chinese art form that involves cutting intricate designs into paper, often creating symmetrical patterns or images of animals, flowers, or cultural symbols. Beijing is known for its paper-cut art, and you can find these delicate and intricate designs in various forms, including wall hangings, greeting cards, and decorative pieces.

Paper-cutting is a highly skilled craft, and the designs can be incredibly detailed, with some pieces requiring hours or even days of work. The art form is often used for celebrations and holidays, such as Chinese New Year, and it holds cultural significance as a symbol of luck and prosperity. If you’re looking for a delicate, intricate, and meaningful souvenir, Beijing paper-cut art is a great option.

9. Chinese Tea Sets

Tea is an essential part of Chinese culture, and a Chinese tea set is a perfect souvenir for tea enthusiasts. Beijing offers a wide selection of tea sets, including traditional Chinese teapots, cups, and tea trays. Many of these sets are made from fine porcelain or clay and are decorated with traditional designs such as flowers, animals, and landscapes.

A Chinese tea set is not just a functional item; it is a beautiful work of art that reflects the country’s long-standing tea culture. Whether you’re a tea connoisseur or simply someone who enjoys a relaxing cup of tea, purchasing a Chinese tea set in Beijing is a great way to bring home a piece of Chinese tradition.

10. Chinese Knotting (Macramé)

Chinese knotting is a decorative handicraft that involves tying knots in a variety of patterns to create intricate designs. These knots are often used to make keychains, jewelry, wall hangings, and other decorative items. The craft is based on the use of Chinese symbolic knots, which are meant to bring good luck, fortune, and protection to those who possess them.

Chinese knotting is an ancient art form that has been passed down through generations. In Beijing, you can find shops selling beautifully crafted items made from colorful threads, including keychains, necklaces, and decorative knots. These items make for unique and meaningful souvenirs, especially for those interested in the symbolism and traditions behind Chinese handicrafts.

Beijing offers a wide range of unique and beautiful handicrafts that reflect the city’s rich cultural heritage. From intricate porcelain and delicate silk embroidery to colorful paper-cuts and traditional lacquerware, the handicrafts available in the city are a testament to the skill and artistry of Chinese artisans. Whether you are looking for a meaningful souvenir or a decorative piece to adorn your home, Beijing’s handicrafts offer something for everyone.
