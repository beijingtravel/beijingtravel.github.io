Are There Hot Springs Worth Trying in Beijing?

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Beijing, the capital of China, is not only a city of historical grandeur and cultural heritage but also a destination where relaxation and wellness await. For those seeking a break from the bustling urban atmosphere, hot springs provide a tranquil escape. Here, we explore some of the best hot springs in and around Beijing, offering details about their unique features, locations, and reasons why they are worth visiting.

Jiuhua Spa & Resort

Located in Changping District, Jiuhua Spa & Resort is one of Beijing’s most renowned hot spring destinations. This resort combines luxurious amenities with natural geothermal waters to create a rejuvenating experience.

Highlights

  • Mineral-Rich Waters: The hot spring water at Jiuhua is sourced from deep underground and is known for its therapeutic properties, rich in minerals like calcium and magnesium.
  • Variety of Pools: Jiuhua features indoor and outdoor pools with varying temperatures, catering to different preferences.
  • Additional Amenities: The resort includes a spa, massage services, and traditional Chinese medicine consultations for a holistic wellness experience.

Accessibility

Jiuhua Spa & Resort is about an hour’s drive from central Beijing, making it a convenient getaway for both locals and tourists.

Price Range

Admission typically costs around 200-300 RMB per person, with additional charges for private rooms or spa treatments.

Longmai Hot Spring Resort

Also located in Changping District, Longmai Hot Spring Resort is another popular choice. Known for its family-friendly environment, this resort caters to guests of all ages.

Highlights

  • Diverse Facilities: In addition to hot spring pools, Longmai offers saunas, a water park for children, and a variety of relaxation areas.
  • Natural Setting: The resort is surrounded by lush greenery, creating a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Cultural Touches: Traditional Chinese architecture and design elements add to the charm of this resort.

Accessibility

Longmai is easily accessible by car or public transport, with shuttle services available from central Beijing.

Price Range

General entry costs approximately 150-250 RMB, with packages available for families and groups.

Xiaotangshan Hot Springs

Xiaotangshan Hot Springs, located in the suburban area of Changping District, is one of the oldest and most famous hot spring areas in Beijing. It has a history dating back to the Ming Dynasty and remains a favored destination for relaxation.

Highlights

  • Historical Significance: This area has been a hotspot for royalty and elites for centuries.
  • Therapeutic Benefits: The waters here are believed to aid in improving skin conditions and relieving joint pain.
  • Affordable Options: Xiaotangshan offers a range of facilities, from luxurious resorts to more budget-friendly options.

Accessibility

Located about 30 kilometers from the city center, Xiaotangshan can be reached by car or local buses.

Price Range

Entry fees vary depending on the venue, ranging from 100 to 500 RMB.

Shunjing Hot Spring Hotel

Shunjing Hot Spring Hotel boasts one of the largest indoor hot spring facilities in Asia. Located within Beijing’s Chaoyang District, this urban hot spring oasis is a convenient choice for those who prefer not to travel far.

Highlights

  • Variety of Pools: The facility features over 80 hot spring pools, each with a unique theme and temperature.
  • International Flavors: Guests can experience different styles of hot spring bathing, including Japanese onsen and Turkish hammam-inspired pools.
  • Dining Options: Shunjing offers a range of dining options, including traditional Chinese cuisine and Western fare.

Accessibility

Situated in the city, Shunjing is easily reachable by taxi or public transportation.

Price Range

Entrance fees are typically around 300 RMB per person, with discounts for evening sessions or group bookings.

Fengshan Hot Springs

Fengshan Hot Springs, located in Fangshan District, offers a more rustic and natural hot spring experience. It is ideal for those looking to connect with nature while enjoying the therapeutic benefits of hot spring waters.

Highlights

  • Scenic Surroundings: Nestled in the mountains, Fengshan provides stunning views and a serene environment.
  • Traditional Experience: The facility emphasizes a connection to nature, with simple yet comfortable amenities.
  • Health Benefits: The mineral-rich waters are said to improve circulation and alleviate stress.

Accessibility

Fengshan is about a two-hour drive from central Beijing, making it suitable for a day trip or an overnight stay.

Price Range

Admission starts at approximately 120 RMB, with additional costs for private baths or rooms.

Yunfoshan Hot Spring Resort

Located in Miyun District, Yunfoshan Hot Spring Resort is known for its eco-friendly design and tranquil setting. It is particularly popular among couples and those seeking a romantic getaway.

Highlights

  • Eco-Friendly Design: The resort incorporates sustainable practices and natural materials into its architecture.
  • Private Pools: Many rooms come with private hot spring baths, offering an intimate experience.
  • Outdoor Activities: Guests can enjoy hiking, cycling, and exploring nearby natural attractions.

Accessibility

Yunfoshan is about a two-hour drive from Beijing and is best reached by car.

Price Range

Room rates start at 800 RMB per night, with access to the hot spring facilities included.

Tips for Enjoying Hot Springs in Beijing

Best Time to Visit

The colder months, particularly late autumn and winter, are ideal for hot spring visits. The contrast between the chilly air and warm waters enhances the experience.

What to Bring

  • Swimwear
  • Towels (though most resorts provide them)
  • Comfortable clothing for relaxation areas

Cultural Etiquette

  • Shower before entering the hot spring pools.
  • Avoid loud conversations to maintain a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Follow any specific rules or guidelines provided by the facility.

Booking in Advance

Many popular hot springs, especially those near Beijing, can get crowded during weekends and holidays. It’s advisable to book in advance to secure your spot.

Beijing’s hot spring offerings provide a perfect blend of relaxation and cultural immersion. Whether you prefer luxurious resorts or more traditional settings, there’s a hot spring experience in Beijing to suit every preference.
