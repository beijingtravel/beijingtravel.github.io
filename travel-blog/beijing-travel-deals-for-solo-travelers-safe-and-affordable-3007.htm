Beijing Travel Deals for Solo Travelers: Safe and Affordable

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Beijing, China’s bustling capital, is a dream destination for solo travelers. With its rich history, modern attractions, and wallet-friendly options, it’s a city that welcomes independent explorers. Whether you're into ancient temples, vibrant street food, or cutting-edge art scenes, Beijing has something for everyone—without breaking the bank. Here’s your ultimate guide to safe and affordable solo travel in Beijing.

Why Beijing is Perfect for Solo Travelers

Safety First

Beijing ranks as one of Asia’s safest cities for solo travelers. With low crime rates, well-lit streets, and efficient public transport, you can explore with peace of mind. Locals are generally helpful, and police presence is strong in tourist areas.

Budget-Friendly Options

From hostels to street eats, Beijing offers incredible value. Many attractions, like the Temple of Heaven or Houhai Lake, are either free or cost under $10. Public transit (subway/buses) is cheap and easy to navigate.

Solo-Friendly Culture

Solo dining is common, and many cafés/co-working spaces cater to digital nomads. Group tours (like the Great Wall hikes) also make it easy to meet fellow travelers.

Top Affordable Attractions for Solo Explorers

1. The Forbidden City (故宫, Gùgōng)

A must-visit, this imperial palace complex costs just $8 (¥60) to enter. Opt for an audio guide or join a free walking tour to dive into its 600-year history. Pro tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds.

2. Hutongs (胡同, Hútòng)

These ancient alleyways are free to wander. Rent a bike ($3/hour) or take a rickshaw tour ($15) to explore hidden courtyards and local tea houses. Don’t miss Nanluoguxiang for quirky shops and snacks.

3. Temple of Heaven (天坛, Tiāntán)

Entry is $5 (¥35), and the park is ideal for solo relaxation. Join locals practicing tai chi or fly a kite in the sprawling gardens.

4. 798 Art Zone (798艺术区)

Beijing’s hipster hub is free to explore. Galleries, graffiti walls, and indie cafés make it perfect for solo photo ops and people-watching.

Wallet-Friendly Eats for Solo Diners

Street Food Gems

  • Jianbing (煎饼): A savory crêpe for $1.50 (¥10) at breakfast stalls.
  • Chuan’r (串儿): Skewered lamb for $0.50 each at night markets like Wangfujing.
  • Baozi (包子): Steamed buns for $2 (¥15) at chains like Bao Yuan Dumpling House.

Solo-Friendly Restaurants

  • Haidilao Hot Pot (海底捞): Known for its solo dining booths and free manicures while you wait.
  • Dali Courtyard: A fixed-menu Yunnan eatery (~$20) with communal tables.

Safe & Cheap Accommodation

Hostels with Social Vibes

  • Peking International Youth Hostel: Dorms from $12/night, plus free walking tours.
  • The Orchid Hotel: Boutique vibes with private rooms from $50 (includes breakfast).

Budget Hotels

  • Holiday Inn Express Beijing Dongzhimen: Central location, rooms from $60/night.

Getting Around Solo

Subway & Buses

A single subway ride costs $0.50 (¥3-9). Download Beijing Subway Map apps for real-time updates.

Bike-Sharing

Use Mobike or HelloBike for $0.30/30 minutes. Just scan the QR code!

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Buy a Transportation Card (Yikatong): Saves 30% on fares.
  2. Visit Free Museums: Like the National Museum of China.
  3. Travel Off-Peak: Winter (Nov–Feb) has fewer tourists and lower prices.

Solo Traveler Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Scams: Ignore "tea ceremony" invites near tourist spots.
  • Rush Hour: Subways get packed at 8 AM/6 PM—plan around it.
  • VPN Prep: Download ExpressVPN before arrival to access Google/Maps.

Final Pro Tip: Learn Basic Mandarin

Phrases like "Nǐ hǎo" (Hello) and "Duōshǎo qián?" (How much?) go a long way. Locals appreciate the effort!

Beijing is a solo traveler’s playground—affordable, safe, and endlessly fascinating. Pack your bags, book that hostel, and get ready for an adventure where history meets modernity at every turn.
